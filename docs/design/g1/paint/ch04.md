## §3 · Bewitchment concept

OSWIN tore the calendar and rinsed the faces out of the town: nobody can FEEL a thing
here, and nobody knows what DAY it is — the two losses are one (no Tuesday, no
looking forward to Tuesday). The town's book-folk stand with blank oval faces; grey
wash sits over everything. `goalDe`: „Gib den Leuten ihre Gefühle zurück — ein
Gefühl nach dem anderen — und häng die Tage wieder auf." `whyDe`: „Jedes Gefühl
bringt eine Farbe zurück ins Buch." Restoration: hue-by-hue return (§1) + faces
re-drawn one feeling at a time (the riff's „restore feelings one by one" made
literal and visible).

## §6 · Guardian arena — der zerrissene Kalender

The hall's giant wall calendar, pages torn and swirling around its frame.
- Pattern: it flings torn day-pages as boomerang arcs (rustle telegraph); on later
  knots it calls a Wecker swarm pulse; between knots the loose pages form brief
  stairs (the arena's own platforms — interlock law).
- **Deflect:** fist a boomerang page back INTO the frame — it pins; stagger.
- **Counter-window tasks:** day-order fixes (pin Monday–Sunday) · be-negative
  rounds („Is it Tuesday?" — „No, it isn't!") · feeling checks on its own face
  („Is he angry? — No, he isn't. He's sad.") · times-of-day matches.
- Cry + console: with all pages pinned, the calendar shows its last torn corner —
  OSWIN ripped out HIS day; nobody ever circled it. The hero draws a red circle
  around today and writes „DEIN Tag". Ally: the camp's calendar — it circles every
  freed friend's day; its cameo later opens a „Sunday door" shortcut.
- Victory: the week hangs whole; the town's bells name the days (restoration heard).
