## §3 · Bewitchment concept

OSWIN's ink pulled the whole class into the book on page one — and here, at the book's
painted schoolhouse, his magic did what SB p.12 already whispered: the school things woke
up angry and the alphabet burst off the board. Letters lie scattered across the meadow
and classroom like dropped confetti; the school things patrol, cross because nobody has
said the friendly words to them yet. `goalDe`: „Sammle die verstreuten Buchstaben und
sag den Schulsachen die freundlichen Wörter — dann findet die Klasse den Weg."
`whyDe`: „Jeder Buchstabe, den du rettest, macht die Tafel wieder heller." Visible
restoration: collected letters fly onto the board rail at the arena; freed beings drop
their scowl-brows and wave.

## §6 · Guardian arena — die erwachte Schultafel

The classroom board itself, wheeled out onto the yard stage, chalk scrawling by itself.
- **Pattern (≤5 knots, telegraph→window per tier):** the board scrawls a WRONG word or
  color-word in chalk (telegraph: the chalk squeaks + the word underlines itself),
  then hurls the chalk piece as a projectile arc. **Deflect** (fist) knocks the chalk
  back and SMUDGES the wrong word — the board staggers (sponge-wipe wobble).
- **Counter-window task types (production, u01-grounded):** fix the board's word —
  imperative commands („Open the door!" ordering), colour question round („What
  colour is the board?"), plural fix (pencil → pencils), letter-order quickfire
  (alphabet run). Window closes with the board's shake-off (readable 2f recovery).
- **The cry + console:** beaten, the board's chalk hand draws a single sad face and it
  weeps chalk-dust tears — it only ever wanted someone to WRITE nice things on it.
  Console beat (choice-less): the hero writes „HELLO :)" — the board blooms it into
  sun-yellow. **Ally tag:** the board becomes the camp's signpost — and late in the
  year (its cameo) flashes hint-words during one hard arena.
- Victory: the collected letters fly onto the board rail (visible restoration), the
  exit sign appears (G11), Klecks stamps the chapter seal.
