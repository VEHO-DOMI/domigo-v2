## 1 · Premise

> **One year after going live honestly, the FOURTEEN crew films the biggest thing that ever happened to their school — a fully paid class trip, destination by student vote: Dublin, New York or Sydney. Then Leo's camera catches a poster that says the winner before the vote begins.** What starts as the channel's first real outside broadcast becomes their first investigation: favors, a sponsor, a teacher they like — and the hardest question of Season 2: *not* "who did it?", but **"how do you tell the truth so it makes things better, not worse?"**

G3 ends on the bridge (ch14: *"No script … They go live. It is messy. It is honest."*). Season 2 cashes it: the crew that stopped scripting now points the camera at the world — and learns that live honesty has rules, costs, and power. Where G3 was about what the internet does to a friend, **G4 is about what the truth does to a community** — and that the teller chooses how it lands.

**Why it's fresh vs G1/G2/G3 (one line):** G1 restored a book, G2 solved a crime with a twist, G3 survived the internet — G4 is the first game where the player *wields* publication: the harm and the repair both come from what the crew chooses to broadcast, and the player's three choices shape who is protected when the truth comes out (it always comes out).

## 3 · The 13-beat outline (chapter = unit; the unit's language IS the beat's substance)

**ACT 1 — The story finds them (ch01–ch05).**
- **ch01 · u01 Welcome to Ireland — "Going Live" (in this PR):** cold-open recap; the assembly — the funded trip + the three-way vote; the crew makes it their first live outside broadcast; corridor interviews run on the unit's own grammar ("What **were you doing** when the news came?"); the class draws **Dublin** to pitch. THE HOOK: Leo's camera catches a poster in the office window — *WINNER: SYDNEY* — a day before the vote opens. Sara's rule holds them back: "Maybe it is nothing. We check first."
- **ch02 · u02 Whodunit — "How to Ask Questions":** the unit is detective fiction — the crew literally learns the method FROM it (motive/opportunity/evidence as reading homework becomes their investigation checklist). First check: the poster was printed on the school printer, ordered by no one who admits it.
- **ch03 · u03 New York, New York — "The Second Pitch":** the NY pitch video (unit vocab carries it) — and the first data anomaly: the "random" pitch assignments gave the loudest classes to Sydney. Leo: "Random does not look like this."
- **ch04 · u04 A working life — "The Newsroom":** work-experience week; `you` and Sara land at the local paper under **Mira Novak**. The craft chapter: two sources, notes, never burn the person who trusts you. Novak smells the story and does NOT take it from them: "It is yours. Do it right."
- **ch05 · u05 Hungry? — "Free Pizza":** the catering trail — SunWays "sponsors" the vote-week snack stand; the crew maps who gets fed and who signs what. Comic surface (Ben reviews the pizza live), serious floor (F-chain: the sponsor's logo is already on the vote posters). Act-1 curtain: this is organized, not an accident.

**ACT 2 — The story tests them (ch06–ch10).**
- **ch06 · u06 Kids make a difference — "Our Voice" + FORK 1:** the moral pivot: does a school channel take on adults? Amelie's naive question ("If the vote is fake, why vote?") decides them. **F1 — HOW to investigate:** `w06.open` (announce the investigation on the channel — pressure, protection, but every door closes) vs `w06.quiet` (work quietly — doors stay open, but the crew carries it alone).
- **ch07 · u07 Travelling Down Under — "The Sydney File":** the Sydney pitch — and the pattern completes: SunWays sells exactly ONE package of the three... Sydney. The unit's travel lexicon carries the file the crew builds.
- **ch08 · u08 Obsessed! — "Leah, 3 a.m.":** the cost chapter. Leah goes past the line (recording a teacher without asking — the G3 lesson resurfacing from the other side). The crew almost breaks; `you` writes the rule on the studio wall: *we are not the story.* Deletes-the-clip beat: the season's most important 10 seconds.
- **ch09 · u09 Body talk — "Before the Interview":** pressure made visible — sleepless Ben, Leah's shaking hands before the first real confrontation request; breathing, preparation, questions on cards. The unit's body/health language is literally the scene.
- **ch10 · u10 A fair world — "The Reason" + FORK 2:** the ethics core. Leo's archive receipt (ch11 seed planted early: Steiner signed the SunWays agreement) + the discovery of his REASON: the sponsor money pays for kids who can't afford ANY trip. Sara: "True and unfair can live in the same sentence." **F2 — HOW to confront:** `w10.private` (show Steiner everything first, alone — he gets the chance to speak, maybe to act) vs `w10.public` (formal on-camera interview request — clean, protected, cold).

**ACT 3 — The story becomes theirs to tell (ch11–ch13).**
- **ch11 · u11 Ready for reading — "The Archive":** the old school mags in the library basement: **SunWays steered a vote before, eight years ago** — and the mag piece about it was never printed (the page is literally missing — an editorial hole as evidence). The crew inherits more than a story: they inherit the unfinished one.
- **ch12 · u12 Space — "The Planetarium":** the school's space-week evening at the planetarium — the whole cast in one room under fake stars. The keystone confrontation happens per F2's staging (Steiner speaks / Brandt deflects); the unit's space vocabulary carries the setting and the metaphor the narrator refuses to overplay: *"From far enough away, everything looks small. We are not far enough away."*
- **ch13 · u13 A school mag — "Print / Live / The Desk" + FORK 3 (three-way):** the crew has the story, proven. **F3 — HOW to publish:** `w13.live` (the live special — maximum truth, maximum blast radius) vs `w13.print` (revive the school mag: the measured special issue, FOURTEEN's name on the masthead — the archive's unfinished piece finally finished) vs `w13.direktorin` (bring the file to Huber first — the institution repairs itself, the crew reports the repair). **The truth comes out in all three.** The epilogue is the honest re-vote, flavored by every flag.

## 5 · Register sheet (binding for every wave line)

- **14-year-old register:** real, quick, a little dry; humor lives in Ben and in cold-open captions; nobody speechifies. ≤2 sentences per line, ≤200 words per scene.
- **No cartoon villains:** Steiner's REASON lands before his exposure (ch10 before ch12 — the W9-before-W10 principle carried over); Brandt is a system, not a devil; Huber is competent; the crew is wrong sometimes (ch08 IS the crew failing).
- **The ethics are shown, never lectured:** Sara's three rules and Novak's craft lines are the only "teaching," each ≤1 sentence, each immediately USED in a scene.
- **Live-honesty doctrine (from G3's ending):** the channel never fakes, never ambushes children, never publishes what it can't prove — and when the crew breaks a rule (ch08), the story makes them pay honestly and repair it on screen.
- **Language discipline:** cumulative bank at each chapter's unit; grammar-taught words (said/told/took, irregular participles) glossed deliberately; `scaffoldDe` is first-class natural teen German (never translationese; the L-1 rule applies at g4 for the toggle path); no meta-talk (VS-8).
- **Every heavy beat ends with the crew** — a hand on a shoulder, Ben's joke, the red REC light going steady. The season's warmth is the friendship, not the outcome.

## 5.1 · Character-stake layer (the identification pass, 2026-07-11)

The scandal plot is the spine; each crew member also carries a **personal stake a 14-year-old recognises** — so the season is character-*driven*, not just well-plotted. Binding for any future revision (do not flatten these back into plot function):

- **Ben — money / class.** Ben is himself one of the twelve names on Steiner's trip-fund list; his family could never afford any trip. Seeded lightly at `ch05.s013` ("two slices to take home — that part is not a joke"), revealed by him at `ch10.s004` ("Mine is one of them"), and paid off at `ch10.s015` ("I am allowed to say that now"). The dignity beat `ch10.s005` (Leo silently slides him the last slice, nobody makes it weird) is load-bearing — never play it for pity.
- **Leah — guilt / becoming-better.** Her ch08 obsession is rooted in Season 1: the crew turned Ben into content, and she swore never to be that person again (`ch08.s005` "Tonight I was her."). Her arc is fear of relapse, not ambition.
- **Sara — protectiveness with an origin.** "People first" exists because she watched the internet nearly break Ben last year (`ch09.s004`). She is not an oracle; the rule has a wound under it.
- **Leo — the quiet kid's medium.** `ch11.s010`: "I film because I talk slowly. A camera waits — people do not." One line, but it turns the camera from a prop into who he is.
- **You — the editor.** Intentionally faceless (player projection); the stake is authorship — your three forks decide who is protected when the truth lands.

The `ch10` reveal deliberately moved the "who the fund helps" explanation off Sara and onto Ben (emotion) — the causal ethics ("sponsor money pays for kids who can't afford trips") are carried by Leah's `ch10.s006` and the `g4u10.ci.the-reason` recap key, so the "true and unfair" logic still lands without exposition. Provenance: `scripts/story/g4-live-character-pass.py`.
