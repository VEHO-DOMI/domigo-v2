# 29 · THE STORY REFOUNDATION — laws, the prologue v2 script, story tasks

**Status: GOVERNING for all story-mode content (2026-07-17). Amends bibles 27/28 (which stay
canon for mechanics + pipeline). Born from Koki's full playthrough verdict: the mechanics core
is right, the storytelling and task fabric around it was a Frankenstein of imported parts.**

The one-sentence law: **the story mode is a story first** — every image, task, and line exists
inside the fiction, introduced before it is used, told at 10-year-old warmth, grounded in the
students' real textbook (MORE! 1).

---

## §1 · REGISTER LAW v2 (German, the story voice)

1. Simple, warm, spoken German. **Banned:** heavy compounds and bureaucratic nouns —
   `verheddert` (→ *verknotet*), `Auftrag` as a header (→ Finn SAYS the goal), plus the sheet
   bans (Monster/Blut/böse/schrei/sterben; threat = leer/weg/still/allein/grau).
2. **Introduce before use.** No proper noun appears before the story has explained it.
   Introduction order (prologue): Frau Berger → das besondere Buch → Finn → Pixel →
   der Tintengeist → Jona → (first door beat:) Siegel, Wächter, Glühwörter.
3. Premises are TOLD BY CHARACTERS in 1–3 short lines — never as a text block. The level goal
   card keeps exactly one warm *Warum-Zeile* (`whyDe`) + one concrete goal line (`goalDe`).
4. German is the DEFAULT story language (grade 1 German-first, L-1); the toggle stays. The
   richness of the storytelling lives in the German scaffold; the English lines stay
   level-true and short (§6).

## §2 · THE CUTSCENE LAW (full-screen picture-book)

- Story beats (prologue, chapter door, chapter restore) play in a **full-screen cutscene
  player**: ONE hi-res illustration fills the frame, the line sits beneath it, tap/Enter/click
  advances, stepped fades between shots. No floating card, no map visible behind.
- **Zero tasks inside cutscenes.** Tasks live in gameplay only (quickfire, seals, boss,
  rescue). The old prologue name-it slot is deleted; the tutorial task is the first in-level
  encounter.
- Cutscene images are **class CUTSCENE** (hi-res painterly, doc 28 two-class law); showing a
  gameplay-resolution sprite full-screen is a defect.
- Replayable: after first viewing, a cutscene is skippable (Überspringen) and re-watchable
  from the map (the book remembers its own story).
- Small in-level NPC lines (Finn's 2–3-line level-open beat, pickup tooltips) stay in the
  compact dialogue card — they are gameplay chrome, not cutscenes.

## §4 · THE STORY-TASK LAW

1. **Never imported.** Story-mode tasks are hand-authored per chapter in the story corpus
   (`keen/chNN.tasks.json`, ids `g1.game.chNN.*`). The general unit library stays for the
   practice surfaces — the game never pulls from it. (Koki's playthrough proof: "What is your
   email address?" is legitimate Unit-1 practice — and absurd as a death-rescue moment.)
2. **A story reason on every task** (`storyDe`): the in-fiction frame line the student reads
   WITH the task. A task without a story reason doesn't ship.
3. **No giveaways**: the answer word never appears in the task's own prompt, its storyDe, or
   the beat lines around its placement (machine-checked, §6).
4. **No meta chrome** in story surfaces: no combo-reset copy, no XP framing, no
   "Vocab Gapfill Level 1" labels. The task card speaks in-fiction ("Das Wort zappelt —
   sag es richtig, dann ist es frei!").
5. **Hint ladder** (the frozen WS-HINT program, adopted natively): every typed task carries
   4 escalation steps — (1) first letter → (2) letter count as underscores → (3) German
   description → (4) the German word. Choice tasks carry steps 3–4 only (1–2 are the task).
6. **Placement**: quickfire = creature contact (choice, fast) · seals = the two in-level seal
   posts (typed, laddered) · boss = the duel's knot phases (typed, laddered) · rescue = the
   Tintengeist hold (choice, gentle dialogue recall — never typed under stress).

- **Door beat** (new scenes s011–s013, staged full-screen; the old s001–s010 stay untouched —
  they belong to the LIVE old-build zone surface): Finn introduces Siegel, Wächter and
  Glühwörter IN STORY, then states the goal. Level header follows:
  `name: "Die verknotete Stunde"`, goalDe rewritten short + concrete, whyDe stays.
- **Restore beat** (new s014–s015): the page turns warm again; forward pull to the next page.
- **Boss register**: intro/taunts lose `verheddert` (→ verknotet); everything else stays.
- **Letter collectibles ARE the Glühwörter fiction**: introduced by the door beat + a one-time
  Finn tooltip on first pickup (PR-D wires it). If a collectible can't be explained in
  fiction, it gets cut — no unexplained Keen imports.
- **Rescue re-script**: the Tintengeist may hold the hero only AFTER its prologue introduction
  (s010). New copy: "Der Tintengeist hält dich fest! Antworte ruhig — dann lässt er los." +
  the two gentle rescue tasks. No email addresses in death phases, ever.

## §6 · THE GROUNDING PIPELINE (English truth)

- Ground truth = the students' actual book: MORE! 1 SB/WB transcriptions (iCloud,
  `Domi Gym 2025:26/1ABC/MORE 1/`). **If in doubt, check the book** — e.g. "storybook" is NOT
  Unit-1 language (the unit says "a short story", "English book"); it dies here.
- `docs/design/g1/grounding/u01-lexicon.json` = the curated Unit-1 word/phrase list (verbatim
  from the transcriptions; word LIST only, no book text in the repo).
- `scripts/check-story-grounding.mjs` (CI-runnable) checks: (a) every EN token in ch00 + the
  new beat scenes + every game task ∈ lexicon ∪ glosses ∪ proper nouns; (b) the giveaway law
  (§4.3); (c) the register bans (§1.1) on the German fields. Red = does not ship.
- Bonus grounding found in the source: Unit 1's own listening text is **"Midnight in the
  classroom" — school things coming alive at night** ("I hate pink!"). The chapter's living
  classroom IS textbook fiction; later waves should mine each unit for such hooks first.
