## The game: "Syntaxia" (designed, not built)

Each of the 13 grammar structures becomes a themed **portal-world**; mastering the structure re-seals the world. The design adds three things beyond the other games' pattern: **real branching** (choice graphs, VS-5-proven dead-end-free — already free in the engine), a **party / hint-economy** (companions unlock by clearing worlds = *derived* from solved items; spendable "Ink" is *cosmetic* and buys scaffolds, never answers), and **boss battles** per world. An optional Three.js "Fog Gate" 3D set-piece is a forfeit-first stretch.

**Three narratives are drafted; Koki is choosing one** (they share the conceit + mechanics, differ in tone):
- **A "The Unmaking"** — regret & redemption (G3-FOURTEEN depth): Cael the Unmaker erasing a painful past; the grammar arc *is* the emotional arc (past-perfect = buried history → reported speech = rumor → third conditional = regret → word-formation = creation).
- **B "The Last Word"** — light heroic romp: relight the worlds + forge the Last Word against the Hush; the lonely Mumble gremlin.
- **C "The Wrong Word"** — G2-style whodunit: who unspoke the Keystone Word; suspects, evidence-tasks, deduction forks, a fair-play twist.

**Why it's low-risk:** branching, the overworld engine (`game-2d`), the story pipeline, and the hint-scaffold seams are all proven on G1/G2/G3; the one genuinely new mechanic is the party/hint-economy, which has clean seams (`task-ui` scaffold hooks + the `context` JSONB + `getSolvedGameItemIds`; no `@domigo/engine` change).
