## 3. The game: "FOURTEEN"

Story files: `content/corpus/stories/g3.st.fourteen/` — `story.json` (14 chapters), `cast.json`, `names.json`, `comprehension.json` (14 `.ci.` items), `release.json` (all 14 live), `art.json`. Code: `packages/game-novel/src/`.

### Premise — the moral engine *is* the design

Four friends start a YouTube channel, **FOURTEEN**, to make English from their book fun. The student (**YOU**) is the channel's **scriptwriter**. Ben's English is shaky; when a video with one of his grammar mistakes goes viral, the group quietly leans into it — Leah engineers **harder lines for Ben so he'll make more mistakes** ("more mistakes, more clicks"). The internet laughs *at* Ben, and **Ben never knows** the mistakes are the point.

The player sits at the exact pressure point: each episode you **fix Ben's line before the video goes live**. The grammar task is literally the act of protecting (or failing to protect) a friend from public ridicule. After the fix, the **comment section renders**, toned by how accurately you protected him. So the mechanic answers a real question every episode — *did your English keep Ben safe?* — and the reward economy (views/subscribers) is the same seduction that corrupts the group. Story-first: **accuracy modulates how much you shielded Ben, never the plot** (the plot is authored; see the band ceiling below). This is Koki's "reward system is solid" reused, not a new pool: the one hidden XP is re-labeled "views," the streak is "trending," each episode banks a subscriber milestone.

### Distinctive mechanic #1 — the comment-section consequence

This is the heart of G3. It lives in `packages/game-novel/src/novel-copy.ts` (`episodeComments`, the comment banks) and `art.tsx` (`CommentSection` renderer); the wiring is in `NovelGame.tsx`.

**How it fires.** A scene's `taskSlot.slot` starting with `fix` (regex `/^fix(-|$)/`, `isFixSlot`) is an on-camera "fix Ben's line" task. When such a task is finished, `NovelGame` flips `showComments`, and instead of a plain Next button it renders `<CommentSection>` before continuing. The comments are chosen by `episodeComments(correctCount, totalFixCount, band)`, where the counts come from the tiers of *this run's* fix tasks (`fixTiers`, only `correct` counts as clean) — an in-session, cosmetic signal, **not** the durable ledger.

**The band ceiling** (`bandForUnit`): unit ≤5 → `warm`, ≤10 → `tense`, else `reckoning`. The band is the *authored emotional ceiling*; accuracy only modulates within it. So the plot is safe from a lucky/unlucky player.

- **WARM (ep01–05).** Clean take → three genuinely kind comments (`KIND`). A slip → mostly kind + one *charmed* tease (`TEASE_WARM`: "his little mistakes are kind of cute tbh"). Even failure reads as the internet finding Ben endearing — *for now*.
- **TENSE (ep06–10).** The channel is now *known* for Ben's slip-ups. Even a **clean** take can't fully undo that — one pitying voice creeps in (`KIND[0]`, `KIND[1]`, `TEASE_TENSE[0]`). A slip → the full `TEASE_TENSE` set, laughs turning pointed ("do they laugh WITH him or AT him?", tone escalating to `cruel`).
- **RECKONING (ep11).** **Cruel regardless of accuracy.** `episodeComments` returns the `CRUEL` bank unconditionally with the line *"You kept this take clean. It doesn't matter — the cruelty has a life of its own now."* The comments ("made a compilation of all his fails 💀", "200k views and they're all laughing AT him") are structural — a clean take changes nothing, and **that futility is the point**. This is the gut-punch the whole arc built toward.

**Where it retires — the `slotPrompt` pivot.** After ep11 the fix-Ben mechanic is **deliberately gone**. Ben is no longer being *performed*, so a "fix his line before it airs" framing would be grotesque. Episodes 12–14 use **neutral, story-true slot prefixes** that (a) get honest, non-channel task labels via `slotPrompt()` and (b) are **not** `fix` slots, so `isFixSlot` is false and **the comment beat never fires**:
  - `truth-*` (ep12, passive voice) → *"✍️ Finish the report — the way it really happened."*
  - `regret-*` (ep13, 2nd conditional) → *"💭 If you could go back — what would you do?"*
  - `promise-*` (ep14, going-to) → *"🎤 Live and honest — help Ben get it right."*
  - `recap` (every episode's `.ci.`) → *"🤔 Did you follow the story?"*
  - `script-*` (ep01–11) → the neutral "write the script" label.

  The result: **after ep11 there is no accuracy-driven cruelty** — the redemption arc can never punish the player's grammar with a cruel comment. This is the single most important coherence property of the whole mechanic and the thing a reviewer should verify hasn't regressed.

### Distinctive mechanic #2 — the production economy (the reward re-skin)

No new points pool (Law 5). The one hidden XP is re-labeled everywhere in `novel-copy.ts`:
- Solving a task = **"a clean take"**; XP shows as **"+N views"** (`resultLine`, computed from `xpForTier`); the channel progress bar reads "Episode views."
- The streak becomes **"trending"** (`trailLabel`: 3 → "🔥 Picking up!", 5 → "🔥 Trending!", 10 → "🔥 Going viral!"), tracked in `sessionStorage` (`domigo:g3:trail`).
- Each finished episode banks a **subscriber milestone** on the upload screen ("the channel just hit N subscribers").
- Result copy is tone-matched: a wrong answer → *"That line aired with a mistake."*; a close/partial → *"Almost — a rough take."* / *"Good — nearly there."*; correct vocab → *"Word locked in!"*, correct grammar → *"Clean take!"*

### Distinctive mechanic #3 — the season board (cumulative hub progress)

`packages/game-novel/src/season-board.tsx` — the G3 analog of the G2 detective Evidence Board, brand-styled in **g3 blue** (not a corkboard). Rendered on `/play/3` as "The season so far." An episode is **"wrapped"** once *every* one of its `taskSlot` items is solved (tier ≠ wrong). Crucially, this is derived server-side from the **authoritative attempts ledger** (`getSolvedGameItemIds`, `page.tsx`), **never** the wipeable cosmetic save (Law 2). Each tile shows ✅ wrapped / ○ released / 🔒 locked; a full season prints *"you saw Ben's whole story through."* The board is a pure render — the server passes derived `EpisodeProgress[]`.

### Distinctive mechanic #4 — the story-comprehension `.ci.` (one per episode)

`comprehension.json` holds **14 `.ci.` items** (id `g3uNN.ci.<slug>.mc.001`), one per episode, all multiple-choice with a German `hintDe`/`explainDe` and per-word glosses. They ride a **`recap` taskSlot** inside each chapter (so they render through the same task renderer, not a separate screen) and check *plot comprehension*, not grammar — e.g. u06 "What does Sara warn you about?" → "Using Ben for fame"; u12 "How does Ben feel?" → "Used and hurt." Difficulty rises 2 → 3 as the moral stakes deepen. They exist so the story load is *checked*, not just presented (the VS-8 spirit — see §5).

### Distinctive mechanic #5 — the art pipeline (decoupled, procedural-first)

`art.json` (`story-art@1`, base `/art/g3`) declares a cover (`cover_title`), an end card (`end_episode`), and a per-chapter card (`card_ch01`…`card_ch14`). `portraits`/`beats`/`clues` are empty. The manifest is **decoupled from disk**: `resolveNovelArt`/`resolveHubArt` resolve only stems that actually exist in `public/art/g3/`, else `null` → the **procedural SVG fallback** in `art.tsx`. `CastAvatar` draws each character deterministically (seeded by a hash of the char key — never `Math.random`, Law 9) with the locked silhouette features (Ben's orange vest, Leah's glasses, Leo's headphones, Sara's silver streak, YOU seen from behind). So the cast reads at a glance even with **zero PNGs shipped** (the current state). The GPT-image prompt library to generate the real ligne-claire panels is committed at `docs/art/g3-fourteen-prompts.html` (PR #71) but has not been run into `public/art/g3/`.

### Runtime / player-flow (`NovelGame.tsx` + `NovelClient.tsx` + `page.tsx`)

- **Hub** (`/play/3`, `page.tsx`, server component, `force-dynamic`): loads the story, released chapters, and derived `solvedItemIds`; renders episode cards (locked → "🔒 Coming soon") + the season board. Chapter N unlocks when its unit is released (`release.json`).
- **Episode** (`/play/3/chNN`): `NovelClient` (client boundary) dynamically imports `<NovelGame>` with `ssr:false` (`loading: "Loading the episode…"`). The server resolves and passes: `chapter`, `castNames`, `storyItems` (the `taskSlot` items pre-resolved), `reviewItems` (a spaced-retrieval hook, currently optional/unused), `serverSave`, and `novelArt`.
- **Inside `NovelGame`:** walks `chapter.scenes` by `id`/`next`. Each scene renders a **comic panel** — narrator (accent-soft italic) or a character (avatar/portrait + name-coloured `inset` shirt accent + the spoken line in a sunken bubble) — with a **🔊 read-aloud** button using `window.speechSynthesis` (en-GB, rate 0.9; no TTS provider needed). Scaffolds are opt-in per scene: a collapsible **German** paraphrase (`scaffoldDe`, "Auf Deutsch?") and **word-glosses** ("Show word help"). Because G3 is A2/A2+, hint scaffolds **fade in the later half**: `fadeHints`/`hideHint` when `chapter.unit >= 8` (the task-ui hint chip is hidden). A scene with a `taskSlot` renders the **one task renderer** (`GrammarItemView`/`VocabItemView` from `@domigo/task-ui`) as `TaskTake`; `Choice[]` `next` renders branch buttons (present in the schema, unused by FOURTEEN, which is linear); a `null` next → the **"Episode uploaded!"** end card with the subscriber milestone.
- **Two persistence paths** (`NovelClient`): graded answers → the **offline attempt outbox** (`sendAttempt`, `mode:"game:g3"`) → `POST /api/attempts` → `@domigo/engine` (XP + Leitner + streak) — so a FOURTEEN answer feeds `/review` like any other. Cosmetic position/`takes` → `localStorage` + a debounced `PUT /api/game-save` (last-write-wins by `clientRev`), flushed on `pagehide`/`visibilitychange`. A **pause-on-blur veil** (classroom-tablet hygiene, Law 9) covers the game when the tab is hidden.

### What a reviewer should scrutinize
1. **VS-8 meta-talk — no "grammar" in student-visible lines.** The whole conceit hides that Ben's *grammar* is the joke. Ben says his "English is not so good" and later "my English is good now" — never "grammar." Spot-check every `textEn`/`scaffoldDe` and every `slotPrompt` label stays in-fiction ("fix Ben's line", "write the script", "finish the report"), never "fix the grammar." The `card`/UI copy is clean today; re-verify after any content edit.
2. **Structure-words-as-vocab.** Probed the current corpus: none of `been / by / let / say / since / for / who / which / whose / unless / could / while / during / until` appear as vocab *lemmas* in any G3 unit — good. But `let` (u09) and passive `by` (u12) *do* appear as structure words in Ben's lines and glosses; if a future wave adds them as vocab items, that's the classic double-count trap. Re-run the probe after any vocab change.
3. **A2 reading load.** G3 is A2/A2+ and the redemption arc (ep12–14) is the heaviest text in the game — passive-voice confrontation, 2nd-conditional regret, going-to promises, plus abstract nouns (fame/shame/content/bullying). Every hard word is glossed and `scaffoldDe` exists, but a reviewer should sanity-check that ep11–14 don't outrun A2+ (the emotional payload is high). The hint-fade at `unit>=8` *removes* the task-ui hint chip exactly where lines get hardest — confirm that's intended, not a difficulty spike.
4. **The consequence's coherence — never cruel-by-accuracy after it retires.** The load-bearing property: (a) the band ceiling means accuracy never changes the plot; (b) ep11 is cruel *regardless* (by design); (c) **after ep11 the fix-Ben mechanic is gone** (`truth-`/`regret-`/`promise-` slots are not `fix` slots, so `isFixSlot` is false and `episodeComments` never runs) — so the redemption can't punish a struggling player with a cruel comment. Verify this invariant holds if anyone renames slots or touches `isFixSlot`/`slotPrompt`. Also worth a pedagogical gut-check: in the **tense** band a *clean* take still yields a mildly pitying comment — intended (the channel's reputation precedes Ben), but confirm it doesn't read as "you did well, here's a punishment."
