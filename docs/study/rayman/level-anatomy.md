# Level anatomy — the 148-image study + the screenshot dispositions (2026-07-20)

**Status: STUDY (W1). Companion to `level-cookbook-v2.md` (whose §8–§10 carry the
transcript laws) and `source-audit-r3.md` (the code-level audit).** Method: every
image in the local wiki library (`~/Code/rayman-study/wiki/`, 148 files across 7
folders) was read and logged individually — four parallel close-reading passes,
then this synthesis. Clean-room throughout: our own words, principles only,
filenames cited in the per-world sections, no art copied, nothing of the studied
game's names/designs enters product content (CP-15).

## The corrections — three beliefs we held that the images overturned

1. **"Levels are fully closed-world" — half true.** Only boss rooms, corridors and
   flight sections are literally closed (root prosceniums, canopies, trunk walls).
   High, open screens close by DEPTH HAZE — a blue atmospheric wall — not painted
   borders. Our law: close the top with hanging flora where the path is low; close
   with haze where the path is high.
2. **"Platforms are thin painted shelves" — wrong for organic worlds.** Every
   forest stand-surface is a thick mass: lit crust over a soil/boulder body
   (crust : body ≥ 1 : 2), floating islands taper to teardrop points, one giant
   prop may BE the floor. Thin hard-edged shelves belong only to the stationery
   world, where thin IS the theme. (Our exercise-book fiction may use both:
   paper-thin where paper, thick-drawn where landscape.)
3. **"Backgrounds are low-saturation silhouettes" — wrong for PC/PS1.** Midgrounds
   are highly saturated; separation works by hue-shift toward blue/teal, lightness
   lift, and soft edges. Only the deepest layer is dark silhouette. Our law:
   separate planes by hue temperature and edge softness — keep backgrounds colorful.

## The synthesis laws (cross-world, for our sheets)

- **Tease-before-grant**: every locked reward is shown screens before its key;
  grants are calm ceremony screens staged on bare ledges with empty sky.
- **The collectible trail is the tutor**: collectibles trace the intended jump or
  flight arc on every teaching screen.
- **One-verb escalation**: a tool teaches itself four times (trivial → over hazard
  → offensive → timed) before the next verb appears.
- **The instrument/object IS the terrain and keeps its anatomy** — a drum walks
  like a drum, a pencil stacks like a pencil; object → platforming-role mapping is
  the heart of each themed world (tables in the per-world sections).
- **Escalate one object, don't add objects.**
- **Every hazard has a face and a telegraph**; facing = flight path; scale is a
  telegraph too.
- **Intensity alternates**: panic set-piece → quiet precision/exploration screen.
- **Weather/palette is the progress dial** across a world (day → clouds → snow →
  storm; the forest literally darkens level by level).
- **Enemies stand ON the path at hero height, one per ledge**; turrets flank gaps;
  canopy hazards threaten safe ground; water/ink is the ambient killer.
- **Bosses: teased safely → pre-drained in a chase → fought in a one-screen flat
  proscenium whose walls narrate the past → REDEEMED** (the sax boss dances; the
  mosquito becomes the mount) — the direct ancestors of our no-kill favor economy.
- **The finale re-tests the whole curriculum by recombination** — your own moves
  mirrored, powers subtracted one by one, fused bosses; nothing new is taught.
- **The world map is ONE painting**; worlds are regions, progression reads as
  altitude, each region = a color field + 2–3 landmark props, the sky slot carries
  story state.
- **HUD: two corner clusters only** (portrait+pips left, one collectible count
  right; boss meter bottom-left in fights); numerals drawn in the game's own hand;
  everything else diegetic.

## The hand dossier (feeds the rig — merged from all four passes)

- Construction: hand = one independent rounded mitten volume, four digits, no
  wrist. **Feet anchor, hands act.**
- **Three hand states only** at sprite scale: ball fist (doing), relaxed curl-mitt
  (neutral), splayed glove (maximum effort/emotion only). A pointing finger exists
  only in poster art.
- **Size: ~⅓–½ of head height in sprites** (bigger only in emotional key art).
- **Open palm = feeling, fist = doing; distance from body = intensity.** Neutral
  keeps each hand within one hand-width of the torso; victory throws both open
  hands a head above the head; the punch removes one hand entirely — two ends of
  one dial.
- **Connectedness without arms — four devices**: one hand overlaps the torso
  silhouette; the far hand is painted one step darker (shared light source); hands
  ride the same motion arc as hair/scarf; compact states tuck the hands fully
  into the silhouette.
- Per-pose signatures: idle = low relaxed mitts at hip, one overlapping the hip ·
  walk = shallow low pendulum · run = front hand closed high at chest overlapping
  the torso, rear hand open low behind (strongest asymmetry) · jump rise = hands
  tuck compact · fall = loose counter-drift · glide = both hands out palms-down
  under the spinning-hair halo (tightrope balance) · charge = fist wound BEHIND
  the hip, sparkling · punch = the fist detaches and flies knuckles-first with a
  trail while the body leans after it · hang/crawl = gloves plant flat and take
  weight like paws · climb = the reaching hand above the head leads · ride = both
  hands planted on the mount · victory = both open hands high and wide (the one
  symmetric pose, reserved for peak emotion).
- Motion craft: hands lead actions by 1–2 frames; loose parts lag (secondary
  motion); the spun hair is drawn as a solid halo-ellipse (smear as object).

## PART B — Level syntheses

Level names and phase structure from the saved wiki pages (`_Pink_Plant_Woods.html`, `_Anguish_Lagoon.html`, `_The_Swamps_of_Forgetfulness.html`, `_Moskito's_Nest.html`, `_The_Dream_Forest.html`). World arc per the overview page: a lush forest that DARKENS as you go through it; swamps encircle the greenery; powers granted in order: telescopic fist (level 1) → ledge-hang (level 2) → grappling fist (level 4).

### 1. Pink Plant Woods (4 parts; part 3 = fist grant, removed on replay)

- **STRUCTURE SKELETON:** One dominant ground band per screen — moss carpet over thick brown soil/stone — with 1–2 floating islands at jump height above it (Foret1PC.png, BalancierPrune.png). Verticality is gentle: single-jump reach. Terraced cliff steps appear late (Explorateurs.png). Closure: bottom is always solid or water-under-moss-lip; sides run off-screen into haze; top is OPEN sky-haze in lagoon-palette screens but visually capped by branch/canopy props. The world reads closed by depth (haze walls), not by literal borders.
- **MECHANIC GRAMMAR:** Part 1 = walk/jump only, no powers, over-signposted (Dreamforest.png signboard). Part 2 shows cages you CANNOT open yet — the game teaches desire before ability (wiki: "ignore all of the cages in this phase"). Part 3 grants the punch; Part 4 immediately re-tests it on 3 cages and hunters. First-screen teaching: collectible orbs laid in an arc exactly along the required jump (Foret1PC.png) — the trail IS the tutorial.
- **TERRAIN DRESSING:** Moss tops get a lighter rim highlight and drip-fringe over every edge (Explorateurs.png); undersides are dark brown earth, rounded, often tapering to teardrop points on floating islands (BalancierPrune.png). Playable plane = highest saturation greens + strongest contrast; midground = saturated but hue-shifted (magenta/purple/orange) and lighter; deep layer = dark teal trunk verticals. Prop density: 3–5 large props per screen (sprout pillar, stump, flower clump, sign), plus ambient butterflies.
- **ENEMY/HAZARD GRAMMAR:** Hunters (Livingstones) stand ON the path at the hero's own height, one per ledge/tier (Explorateurs.png, ExplorateurGrimace.png); they telegraph by silhouette (tall hat + rifle shape) and face-direction. The grimace scares small ones pre-power (wiki). No aerial enemies in level 1 imagery.
- **SECRETS/CAGES:** Cages are shown to the player before they're reachable — the return-trip pattern is the level's spine (wiki parts 2 vs 4). Bonus levels hang off parts 2 and 4.
- **SET-PIECES & PACING:** Fairy grants are staged as calm, enemy-free "ceremony screens" with the densest background painting (BetillaTelescopicFist.png). Rhythm: teach → tease → grant → test.
- **CONTRADICTS PRIOR BELIEF:** platforms are NOT thin shelves — every stand surface is a thick mass (soil body ≥ 2× the moss crust).

### 2. Anguish Lagoon (4 parts: hop course → Bzzit boss → flight ride → hang grant)

- **STRUCTURE SKELETON:** Water line becomes a structural floor: small moss islands and lily pads float ON the water band; the path oscillates between island line and flower-top line (Nenuphars.png). Part 2 is a vertical drop INTO a boss pocket (wiki: "fall from the top of the level"). Part 3 is a horizontally flown corridor closed at the TOP by berry-vine canopy (DosBziiit.png).
- **MECHANIC GRAMMAR:** Introduces water-as-death + moving/organic platforms (lily pads, plums). Plum mechanics escalate: stand on plum (BalancierPrune.png) → plum over spike carpet (PruneFruitsPiquants.png) → plum onto enemies (ExplorateursPrunes.png). Post-boss the level flips genre for one part: rail-flight with an orb trail as the steering guide (DosBziiit.png).
- **TERRAIN DRESSING:** Lagoon palette: misty blue-lavender lake wash, distant mountains/volcano, palm silhouettes IN HAZE (the closest thing to the "silhouette background" belief — but they are hazy-blue, not grey, and midground plants stay fully saturated). Causeways = rounded boulder rows capped with moss (Lianepiquante.png).
- **ENEMY/HAZARD GRAMMAR:** Carnivorous flowers as fixed turrets flanking gaps at two heights (Nenuphars.png); red spike-stars dress ledge edges as no-stand markers; thorn vines hang into the path from the canopy (Lianepiquante.png) — hazards come from above over safe ground, or sit at edges. Bzzit hovers at path height, telegraphing with its long needle nose (BziiitPC.png).
- **SECRETS/CAGES:** 5 cages in part 1 alone, one locked behind a power from TWO levels later (wiki) — cross-level return trips, not just within-level.
- **SET-PIECES & PACING:** The emotional centerpiece of the world: beaten boss CRIES, hero consoles it, it becomes the mount for part 3 (wiki + DosBziiit.png). Fight → tears → friendship → shared flight. (This is the redemption template the Painted Book pivot builds on.)
- **HERO NOTE:** Riding pose — hero sits upright on the mount's back, hands resting forward on its head/neck (DosBziiit.png).

### 3. The Swamps of Forgetfulness (3 parts: flood escape → plum-cage hunt → spike-field crossing)

- **STRUCTURE SKELETON:** Darkest palette of the world (the wiki's "forest darkens" arc is literal): night blues, dark teal trunk walls filling BOTH side borders (Plante.png, Fleurtentacule.png). Part 1 is a vertical shaft climbed on grown flowers while the water floor RISES (Plante.png). Parts 2–3 return to horizontal causeways over piranha water (Piranha.png).
- **MECHANIC GRAMMAR:** Part 1 swaps a core verb for one level: the magic seed replaces the grimace; you PLANT your own platforms under time pressure from the flood (wiki + Plante.png). Part 3 stress-tests the plum: balance-roll it across a continuous spike-fruit carpet (PruneFruitsPiquants.png). The countdown timer appears (Plantecarnivore.png "0:03").
- **TERRAIN DRESSING:** Rain streaks + wave band animate the hazard floor (Plante.png); stone-ball causeways with moss caps standing in black water; giant fallen logs serve as whole-screen floors with fungus lumps as bumps (Tronc.png). Undersides here are stacked stones, clearly drawn, water lapping at them.
- **ENEMY/HAZARD GRAMMAR:** Piranhas leap in fixed arcs at raft crossings (Piranha.png); tentacle-flowers guard island gaps (Fleurtentacule.png); giant carnivorous plants occupy stump-tops as area denial (Plantecarnivore.png). The water itself is the primary enemy.
- **SECRETS/CAGES:** "Plums are very useful in terms of finding the cages" (wiki) — cages sit above plum-boost spots; one PC cage needs a power from world 2 (helicopter) — the game plants a cross-WORLD return trip.
- **SET-PIECES & PACING:** Tarayzan intro gag (knock down the loincloth, get the seed — Ta-Ray-Zan.png) → immediate flood panic → calm cage-hunt breather → spike-field skill test. Panic first, precision last.

### 4. Moskito's Nest (6 parts: cage intro → nenuphar gauntlet → CHASE → trunk maze → boss → grapple grant)

- **STRUCTURE SKELETON:** Recombines every prior structure at higher tempo: nenuphar/flower-spring gauntlet with SWINGING spike-fruits (wiki part 2), then a linear chase corridor (MoskitoPoursuite.png), then a giant-trunk maze (wiki part 4, PC-only trunks like Tronc.png), then the fully closed boss proscenium (MoskitoFight2.png: branch floor, root walls, misty light-shaft center).
- **MECHANIC GRAMMAR:** Nothing new until the grant at the end — the level is the world's exam. The chase inverts control: the threat sets the pace (huge spiked fruit pushed at you from the left, MoskitoPoursuite.png). The boss reuses the mini-boss's moveset writ large (needle dive, fruit drop) — grammar learned on Bzzit pays off on Moskito.
- **TERRAIN DRESSING:** Deep-forest palette (dark teal trunk walls) with mossy causeways; the boss arena is the most "painted room" screen in the world — every border filled with curving root/branch masses, a glowing atmospheric center instead of a sky.
- **ENEMY/HAZARD GRAMMAR:** Swinging spike-fruits as pendulum hazards over the platform chain (wiki part 2); the boss telegraphs with hover-then-dive and the spiked fruit's sheer size (half the screen, MoskitoPoursuite.png) — scale IS the telegraph.
- **SECRETS/CAGES:** Part 1 shows a cage needing the grappling fist granted in part 6 of the SAME level — the tightest tease-then-grant loop in the world. Parts 3 and 5 (chase, boss) vanish on replay; part 4 has NO cages (pure traversal).
- **SET-PIECES & PACING:** gauntlet → chase (max intensity) → quiet trunk maze (breather) → boss → ceremony. Intensity alternates screen by screen.

## PART C — Hero poses & hands (forensic, for the hand-animation rework)

Body model (RaymanLimbs.jpg sketch + all gameplay shots): head, torso, two floating mitten HANDS, two floating shoe FEET — no arms, no legs, ever. The hands do all expressive work.

- **Scale:** each glove ≈ 0.8–1.2× head height in artwork (Rayman_Cheat_Artwork_1/2, splash), ≈ 0.6–0.8× in in-game sprites (Foret1PC.png). Gloves are white, 4 digits (3 fingers + thumb), drawn as a rounded mitten mass with fingers cut in only when spread.
- **Idle:** hands hang at mid-torso height, slightly OUT from the body sides (a gap of about half a hand-width — nothing connects them), fingers relaxed together (SpaceMama.png left figure, Foret1PC.png). Tutorial/ceremony idle: both hands raised chest-high, palms out, fingers spread (BetillaTelescopicFist.png).
- **Run:** hands pump like a sprinter's — lead hand loosely closed at chest height ahead of the torso, trailing hand swung BEHIND the torso at hip height; body tilts forward; feet split in a long stride (MrSax.png, MoskitoPoursuite.png, RRR1_GBA_Livingstone.png).
- **Jump/leap:** both hands rise above shoulder line, open, fingers spread wide — the silhouette flares (BziiitPC.png top-left, Explorateurs.png top-left). Hands lead the arc.
- **Balance (on plum):** arms-out tightrope pose — both hands out at shoulder height to the sides, palms down, fingers slightly spread (BalancierPrune.png, PruneFruitsPiquants.png).
- **Punch:** the fist DETACHES and flies as its own sprite — a clenched white fist, knuckles forward, thumb on top (Poing.png); the body leans into the throw, the remaining hand trails behind for counter-balance. Charge-up: the fist winds BEHIND the body at hip height with sparkles on it (MrDark.png).
- **Ride:** seated upright on the mount, both hands planted forward on the mount's head (DosBziiit.png).
- **Crouch/brace:** body compressed, both hands pulled in FRONT of the chest, close together (Skops.png).
- **Expressive/story:** shush — index finger to lips, other three digits curled (splash screens); open-palm wave with all four digits clearly separated (Rayman1Group.PNG, Rayman_Cheat_Artwork_2.png).
- **Law for the rework:** every pose reads at silhouette level through HAND POSITION alone (ahead = action, above = jump, out = balance, behind = charge/run, in front = fear). Fingers only articulate for expression (shush, wave, spread); locomotion uses closed mitten masses.

## PART D — Contradictions of prior beliefs (all three partially wrong)

1. **"Levels are fully closed-world" — HALF TRUE.** Boss arenas and the flight corridor are literally closed (root prosceniums MoskitoFight2.png; berry canopy DosBziiit.png; trunk walls Plante.png). But lagoon-palette screens have OPEN hazy sky/lake uppers (Foret1PC.png, Nenuphars.png, PruneFruitsPiquants.png) — closure there is done by DEPTH HAZE (a blue atmospheric wall), not painted borders. Rule to adopt: close the top with hanging flora where the path is low; close with haze where the path is high.
2. **"Platforms are thin painted shelves" — WRONG for the forest.** Every stand surface is a thick mass: moss crust over a deep soil/boulder body (Explorateurs.png, Lianepiquante.png), teardrop floating islands (BalancierPrune.png), whole-log floors (Tronc.png). Thin hard-edged shelves exist only in Picture City (Image1PC.png) where "thin" is the theme. Forest rule: crust : body ≥ 1 : 2, underside always drawn.
3. **"Backgrounds are low-saturation silhouettes" — WRONG for PC/PS1.** Midgrounds are HIGHLY saturated (magenta corals BziiitPC.png, purple mushrooms BetillaAccrochage.png); separation is by hue-shift (toward blue/teal), lightness lift, and soft edges — not desaturation. Only the deepest layer (trunk verticals) and the GBA port (Dreamforest.png flat lilac + silhouettes) match the old belief. Rule: separate planes by hue temperature and edge softness; keep the background COLORFUL.

## PART E — Extracted level-design laws (for @domigo/game-paint)

1. **Tease-before-grant:** show every locked reward (cage) screens or levels before the ability that opens it; grants are ceremony screens; replays delete grant/boss parts.
2. **The collectible trail is the tutor:** orbs laid in arcs ALONG the intended jump/flight line teach every new traversal beat without text (Foret1PC.png, RaymanPC_-_Jungle_21.png, DosBziiit.png).
3. **Thick-crust terrain:** moss crust with lit rim + drip fringe over a dark thick body; floating islands taper to points; one giant prop may BE the floor for a screen.
4. **One-verb escalation chain:** introduce a tool trivially (stand on plum), then over hazard (spike carpet), then offensively (onto enemies), then under time pressure — same object, four lessons.
5. **Hazard placement grammar:** enemies stand ON the path at hero height (one per ledge); static turret-plants flank gaps; hazards over safe ground hang from the canopy; edges are marked with spike-stars; water is the ambient killer.
6. **Intensity alternation:** panic set-piece (flood, chase) is always followed by a quiet precision or exploration screen; the world literally darkens across its four levels.
7. **Redemption loop as mechanic:** boss → tears → consolation → ally-mount with its own traversal part (the direct ancestor of the Painted Book favor-economy).

## 1. BAND LAND — per-level study (our "Our Band" 1:1 analog)

World logic (from `_Band_Land.html` + all images): the ground is drum skin or slippery
sheet-music staves; instruments are the platforms; the hazards are wrong notes, spiky
balls, trumpets, mallets, cymbals; altitude increases level by level, bringing clouds →
snow → lightning. Level order: Bongo Hills → Allegro Presto → Gong Heights → Mr Sax's
Hullaballoo.

### 1.1 Bongo Hills (6 parts — the long "learn the world" level)

STRUCTURE SKELETON
- Part 1 (PC): a literal TRANSITION level — xylophone/keyboard bridges over quiet water,
  green forest hills still visible behind (`Ciel1PC.png`). The old world's palette carries
  into the new world's objects; the player climbs up and "arrives".
- Parts 2–6 alternate between GROUND BANDS (drum-skin floors with decorated rims —
  `Mite.png`, `MiniNotes.png`) and open-air object chains (cork discs `LightningEyes.png`,
  maraca flight `MaracasRocket.png`, the sprinkler wheel `Tourniquet.png`).
- Vertical rhythm: pt 3 is a vertical ascent (maraca rockets), pt 4 a horizontal storm
  corridor, pt 5 a rotating-hazard gauntlet, pt 6 a flat ground-band finale with the
  biggest enemy (`RedMonster.png`). Long level = alternating traversal verbs, not more
  of the same verb.

MECHANIC GRAMMAR
- Pt 1 teaches "instruments are floors" with zero hazards (sagging key-bridge over water).
- Pt 2 introduces moths (tough flyer) and wrong notes with a jump-over rule; a collectible
  string that plays a song when taken in order (wiki) — collecting IS music.
- Pt 3 introduces the maraca rocket (mount) plus spiky balls as the cost of steering error.
- Pt 4 introduces the lightning turret (`LightningEyes.png`) — first ranged hazard.
- Pt 5 stress-tests: sprinkler hub + orbiting spike-notes + wrong notes below
  (`Tourniquet.png`) — timing wheel over a punish floor.
- Pt 6 releases tension: flat band, one huge slow enemy, no cages (wiki).

TERRAIN DRESSING
- Drum floors: pale cream top (walk surface) → patterned rim (diamond quilting, gold
  studs) → crossed lacing beneath (`Mite.png`, `MiniNotes.png`, `MrSaxAllegroPresto.png`).
  The underside is always MORE decorated than the top; the top stays plain so the hero
  and hazards read.
- Play plane saturated and hard-edged; background hills/instrument silhouettes soft and
  desaturated (`Ciel1PC.png`, `Flute_Tree_Tall.png`). Foreground props (flowers, pebbles)
  sit strictly below the walk line.
- Landmark flora = instruments as trees (`Flute_Tree_Tall.png`).

ENEMY/HAZARD GRAMMAR
- Wrong notes: living note-creatures, sharp tails, placed as air walls or floor pits
  (`FaussesNotes.png`, `MiniNotes.png`).
- Spiky balls: blue crystal stars, in clusters and orbits (`Tourniquet.png`).
- Moths/mites: durable on-path walkers/flyers (`Mite.png`).
- Turrets: fixed face + horizontal bolt (`LightningEyes.png`).
- Escalation by size: mini-notes → moths → red monster (`RedMonster.png`).

SECRETS/CAGES — wiki: numerous secret passageways in pt 1 (console versions); pt 5 has
two secret areas ("one much harder"); pt 4's cage is placed to reward returning WITH the
helicopter power — secrets are power-gated re-visits, not pixel hunts.

SET-PIECES & PACING — maraca-rocket flight (pt 3) and the sprinkler wheel (pt 5) are the
two spikes; a song-string collectible is the pt 2 charm beat; storm palette marks pt 4.

### 1.2 Allegro Presto (5 parts — the "slippery staves" level)

STRUCTURE SKELETON — almost NO ground bands: the level is floating stave-ribbons (short
wavy ice-blue strips, `Ciel1PS.png`, `TrompetteSaut.png`) over void, strung between giant
vertical instruments (treble-clef tower, flute columns). Pt 3 is "over a huge void" (wiki).

MECHANIC GRAMMAR
- Pt 1 introduces SLIDING: the stave surface is slippery; the first ribbons are wide and
  landing-safe, later ones short and angled.
- Pt 2 introduces trumpets as air movers (suck/blow — `TrompetteSaut.png`): aim = bell
  direction; only wrong notes + mallets accompany them (one new verb, minimal noise).
- Pt 3 adds swinging mallets over the void (`HittingStick.png`) and ends by bouncing off
  a monk — deliberately foreshadowing the NEXT level's cast (wiki).
- Pt 4 = boss tease: Mr Sax blows wrong notes, hero punches ONE back, boss retreats
  (`MrSaxAllegroPresto.png`) — teaches the entire boss mechanic in one safe exchange.
- Pt 5 = helicopter power grant (`BetillaHelicoptere.png`), with one free life hidden by
  falling between a flute beside the exit sign (wiki) — the FIRST helicopter secret is
  right next to where you learn it.

TERRAIN DRESSING — cold palette flip (pink/blue dusk vs Bongo's green day); chrome and
glass materials (the clef tower) against warm wood/brass; ribbons have a bright top edge
and soft blue body — "ice" read without any ice.

ENEMY/HAZARD GRAMMAR — mallets swung by gloved hands from columns with telegraph eyes
(`HittingStick.png`); wrong-note projectiles from the sax; slipperiness itself is the
main enemy.

SET-PIECES & PACING — slide runs → trumpet flight → void mallets → boss tease → power
grant: a rising-tension line released by a gift.

### 1.3 Gong Heights (no dedicated page saved; from `_Band_Land.html` + images)

- "A balmy region of golden clouds inhabited by meditating monks" — altitude zone above
  Allegro Presto (`MoineBongosHaut.png`: orange-gold haze, maraca hot-air balloon).
- Monks are giant seated characters used as living springboards/bounce platforms (the
  Allegro pt 3 hand-off proves the mechanic); bongos "cross chasms", gongs/cymbal
  platforms hang in cloud space (`Cymbales.png` style objects).
- Traversal verb = riding tiny flying clouds (`MoineBongosHaut.png`, `RedMonster.png`)
  and bouncing drum-to-drum; per `_The_Hard_Rocks.html`, Gong Heights pt 2 has an
  invisible force-scroll chase — the game's first auto-scroll pressure section.
- Law for us: the calm-looking level hides the first timed-scroll challenge; monks =
  friendly NPC-shaped mechanics (a person you bounce off, not an enemy).

### 1.4 Mr Sax's Hullaballoo (3 parts — the exam + boss)

- Pt 1 = the WORLD EXAM: "features almost everything that can be found in Band Land"
  plus one new platform (the cymbals, `Cymbales.png`) — remix every taught mechanic,
  add exactly one novelty, and hide ALL SIX cages here (wiki). Weather beat: it starts
  snowing toward the end — altitude/pacing marker before the boss.
- Pt 2 = chase across cloud banks (`MrSaxPoursuite.png`): the pursuing boss spits wrong
  notes; punching them back pre-drains his health (up to 9 of 12 pips) — the chase is a
  skill-check with a tangible boss-fight reward.
- Pt 3 = arena (`MrSax.png`): round tiled chamber, central pagoda structure, boss 4×
  hero; defeat ends in a DANCE together (wiki) — the boss is redeemed, not destroyed.
  This is the single best precedent for our no-kill redemption law.

## 2. BLUE MOUNTAINS — per-level study

World logic (`_Blue_Mountains.html` + images): a climb-world — chasms, abrupt slopes,
floods and snow tempests; hazards are spiked ground, flying picks, giant spiky balls;
enemies are stone men and stone dogs (terrain come alive); the musician NPC story runs
through it; powers: Super-helicopter potion (from the musician) and running (from the
fairy at the end).

### 2.1 Twilight Gulch (2 parts)

- STRUCTURE: dusk-lit diagonal ridges with glowing moss-strip tops (`Montagnes1PC.png`),
  knob outcrops at varied heights (`ChiensdePierre.png`), pendulum spike-balls over the
  slope lanes (`GrosseBoulePiquanteBalancier.png`).
- MECHANICS: pt 1 is short and introduces both new enemy families — stone men (big,
  boulder-throwing: `HommedePierre.png`, `HommePierreRit.png`) and stone dogs (small,
  pop-up). Requires an earlier world's power (grappling fist) to proceed, and one cage
  needs the future running power (wiki) — deliberate cross-world power-gating. All six
  cages live in pt 1.
- Pt 2 = the Mr Stone CHASE (`MrStonePoursuite.png`): sheltering rhythm — run, duck into
  a crevasse, wait out the pursuer, run again; spike carpets under the jump line.
- HAZARD GRAMMAR: throwers stand across the gap you must jump; spike-balls swing across
  slopes on visible chains; punchable spike-balls (wiki) turn a hazard into a toy.

### 2.2 The Hard Rocks (3 parts — the trap level)

- Known for SUDDEN TRAPS tied to trigger-doors (wiki: "gendoors") — invisible trigger
  lines that spawn/release hazards. Cave corridors with stalactite ceilings and thin
  mossy ledge strips (`Pieu.png`); vertical shafts guarded by face-stakes pointing down
  (`Pieux.png`); stone-men pincers (`DeuxHommesPierre.png`).
- Pt 1 has TWO exit signs — a fake-out exit leading to a guarded bonus (wiki): exits
  themselves become secrets.
- Pt 2 has two forced-scroll "invisible force" sprints (the world's speed exam).
- Pt 3 compensates high trap difficulty with many hidden bonus lives (wiki) — cruelty is
  paid back in secrets.
- HAZARD GRAMMAR: stakes/picks are wooden darts WITH EYES (facing = flight direction),
  ducked under or threaded between; traps trigger on approach lines, but every hazard
  still has a face and a fixed pattern.

### 2.3 Mr Stone's Peaks (6 parts — story + weather + boss)

- Pt 1 = NPC problem tableau (`Musicien.png`): the musician's tree-house, his guitar
  crushed under a boulder drawn right into the scene; helping him earns the

## 5. Cross-world design laws (the strongest, ranked)

1. **The instrument IS the terrain, and every terrain piece keeps its instrument anatomy** —
   walk surface plain, rim/underside decorated (drum skin/rim/lacing; key/rail; stave-edge).
   Objects never become abstract platforms; they stay drums, keys, staves at one glance.
2. **One new verb per part, taught in safety first** — pt 1 of every level is hazard-light
   and shows the new object in a consequence-free frame (key-bridge over water; wide
   staves; short stone-men intro); the same verb is stress-tested 2–3 parts later; the
   final level of a world is a remix exam with exactly ONE novelty (Hullaballoo's cymbals).
3. **Every hazard has a face and a telegraph** — eyes between cymbal plates, eyes on
   stake tips (facing = flight path), eyes on lightning columns, laughing throwers,
   glow-cracks on boulders. Nothing hurts without first showing a readable "alive" cue.
4. **Play plane vs background: saturation + edge contrast** — the walkable lane is bright,
   hard-edged, lip-highlighted (moss strips, cream drum tops); backgrounds are soft
   desaturated silhouettes of the same vocabulary; foreground props live strictly below
   the walk line. Giant one-off landmarks (clef tower, flute tree, carved face, shield)
   orient the player inside repeating texture.
5. **Bosses are teased, pre-fought, then redeemed** — early cameo teaches the counter
   (punch the note back), the chase lets you pre-drain the boss, the arena is symmetric
   with a central structure, and victory ends in a dance — the enemy joins the world
   rather than leaving it. (Plus the musician: help first, power as thanks.)

Secondary laws worth keeping: weather = progress dial (day → clouds → snow → lightning,
snow starting mid-level before a boss); power-grant beats on bare flat stages; secrets =
power-gated revisits, fall-through gaps beside exits, fake exit signs; cruelty repaid in
hidden lives; collectibles that sing when taken in order.

## 6. Pacing spine of a Rayman world (for Our Band's chapter plan)

1. Long teaching level, 6 parts, alternating traversal verbs, transition scenery from the
   previous world in part 1 (Bongo Hills).
2. Skill level built on ONE surface property (slipperiness), boss tease + power grant at
   the end (Allegro Presto).
3. Atmosphere/altitude level with a friendly-NPC mechanic and the first timed-scroll
   pressure (Gong Heights).
4. Exam level: everything remixed + one novelty + all cages + weather turn → chase →
   arena → dance (Mr Sax's Hullaballoo).

## 7. Hero poses & hands — forensic notes (for the hand-animation rework)

Character build: floating hands and feet, no arms/legs; hands are WHITE GLOVES that read
as independent actors. At sprite scale each hand is a 3–5 px white blob, so POSE is
carried by hand POSITION, not finger detail; finger detail exists only in promo art.

- **Promo hands (`Band_Land.png`)**: playing the piano — near hand SPLAYED flat over the
  keys, thumb separated wide, fingers spread and slightly bent at the knuckle line;
  far hand a loose fist mid-strike. Glove is plump, cartoon-inflated; each finger has
  visible volume and a soft shadow crease at its base. Hand size ≈ the hero's whole face.
- **Promo hands (`Blue_Mountains.png` / `R1_JP_Demo_...png`)**: panic tumble — BOTH hands
  thrown up beside the head, palms out toward the viewer, fingers spread to maximum
  (star-shape), thumbs down-angled. With no arms, the raised open hands alone carry the
  emotion; mouth wide open supports it. Hands again face-sized, drawn larger than feet.
- **Idle/stand (`Cymbales.png`, `Bouclier.png`, `Fleur.png`, `Musicien.png`)**: hands hang
  as small rounded fists at hip height, floating a constant small gap from the body
  sides, slightly in front of the torso plane.
- **Run (`ArbreNoueux.png`, `Ciel1PC.png`, `MrSaxAllegroPresto.png`, `Pieu.png`)**: torso
  leans forward, legs scissor wide; hands PUMP alternately — one fist forward at chest
  height, the other trailing behind the back line; hair flag streams backward.
- **Jump ascent (`Pieux.png`, `MrStonePoursuite.png`)**: both hands fly UP and OUT above
  shoulder height, open (fingers suggested by notched blob edges), legs split
  front-and-back or tucked; hair lifts vertically. The open raised hands are the "in
  the air" silhouette signature.
- **Helicopter descent (`Ravin.png`, `ChiensdePierre.png`)**: hair spins into a flat
  yellow halo-disc above the head; hands held out at mid-height to the sides, relaxed
  half-open, palms down (a balancing pose); feet dangle together pointing down.
- **Climb/leap-up (`EauMonte.png`)**: body vertical, near hand REACHING above the head
  toward the next ledge (the reaching hand leads the pose), far hand low for balance.
- **Riding a mount (`MaracasRocket.png`, `La_bouingue.jpg`)**: both hands forward
  gripping the mount's front edge, body crouched low over it, hair flattened back.
- **Cloud-lounge (`MoineBongosHaut.png`, `RedMonster.png`)**: crouched/kneeling compact on
  a tiny cloud, hands tucked near the front knee — a "passenger" silhouette.
- **Attack (from the gloved-hand mallet `HittingStick.png` + boss-counter mechanic)**: the
  world's own hazards use a WHITE GLOVED FIST holding a mallet — the hero's fist and the
  world's hands share one glove design language; the punch is a thrown fist that leaves
  the body (so the attack pose is body braced, remaining hand raised as guard).

Rework implications: (a) make hands ~face-sized and always visibly separated from the
body; (b) give every state ONE unmistakable hand position (idle = low fists, run =
alternating pump, jump = both up open, helicopter = out-palms-down, climb = one-up-reach,
ride = both-forward-grip); (c) reserve splayed-finger detail for large/promo frames and
emotional spikes; (d) let hands lead emotion — panic, triumph, effort are hand poses
first, face second.

## PART A — PICTURE CITY (the stationery world; our closest cousin)

### A0. World fiction (from `_Picture_City.html` + `Picture_City.png`)

The manual frames it as "the world of images... and mirages": the land is literally built of drawing materials, and twice the hero crosses a **theatre stage** where painted backdrops (space set, pirate-beach set) become the fight arena. `Picture_City.png` is the emblem: a famous-painting parody inside a heavy gilded frame, with a tiny painter figure and paint pots at the frame's corner — the frame itself is a physical place you stand on. **Law A0: the fiction openly admits it is a picture.** The world never pretends to be nature; the artifice is the charm. This is exactly our "drawn into the exercise book" premise, one generation earlier.

### A2. Terrain dressing laws (how it stays readable)

1. **Solid = saturated + hard dark outline; background = desaturated wash.** In `Image1PC.png` the platform slabs are crisp two-tone blocks with black edging; the sky behind is soft violet paint-waves with zero outline. You always know what you can stand on.
2. **Sides and undersides carry the "material story."** Slab sides get decorative zigzag bands (`Craies.png`), checkered trim (`BacEncre.png`), ink dribbles (`Image1PC.png`); wooden planks show grain + screws on the face (`Bois.png`). Tops stay clean for gameplay; storytelling lives on the vertical faces.
3. **The desk-wood backdrop carries child-graffiti.** `TubeGouache.png` background wood has faint chalk arithmetic ("8x8"-style scribbles, circles) — the world doodles on itself. Directly stealable for our book: faint pencil margin-scribbles behind the play plane.
4. **Hazard grammar is color-coded by material:** white/silver sharp things (pins, nibs) = spikes; black liquid = death; sparkle-on-navy = bounce; ink-stain-on-slab = slippery. One glance, one meaning, no text.
5. **Foreground props overlap the play plane sparingly** (brush handles, palette edges) to sell depth without hiding footing.

### A3. Level: ERASER PLAINS (4 parts; French name = "The Creek of Pencils")

- **Structure skeleton:** P1 = bobbing-pencil crossing of an ink ocean, capped by a ceiling of drawing pins that forces speed at the end. P2 = shrink-potion rooms (hero shrunk twice to pass small gaps) + running under "waves of giant pencils." P3 = variation of P2 with a bonus level; the Jaguar version replaces it with a flying-ring gauntlet + the sparkle-marked checkerboard death-puzzle. P4 = first boss bout on the theatre stage.
- **Mechanic grammar:** the level INTRODUCES yin-yangs (throwable stepping helpers) in P1, shrinking in P2 (`MiniRayman.png` — two body scales on the same block terrain, blocks become architecture when small; note the block with a tiny "1" pip sticker, dice/domino faces used as tile decor), and the first stationery-world enemies in P2/P3.
- **Enemy/hazard grammar:** pins are placed ABOVE the pencil path (punish dawdling, not jumping); space-pirate enemies appear only from P2 on, after the movement lesson is done. Small tick-like ground bugs (`Craies.png`) fill flat stretches.
- **Set-piece:** the checkerboard sparkle-puzzle (`EP_puzzle.png`) — a grid wall where marked squares are safe and unmarked ones hide pin-boxes; observation-before-commitment as a set-piece. Superb for our book: a grid of exercise-book squares where only the "correct answers" hold weight.
- **Pacing:** water-crossing rhythm → shrink novelty → same-but-harder → boss. Novelty per part, one at a time.

### A4. Level: PENCIL PENTATHLON (3 parts; French name = "Thumbtack Panic")

- **Structure skeleton:** P1 = forced-speed run across ink-covered (slippery) erasers over void. P2 = free-flight (super-helicopter potion) through corridors bristling with pins, pencils, and fountain-pen nibs. P3 = descent down a tall shaft of bouncing + slippery erasers where "all moves have to be calculated" (`GommesRebond.png` is this fabric: navy glitter bouncers stacked in a shaft).
- **Mechanic grammar:** this is the STRESS-TEST level — no new verbs, but each part takes one already-taught surface (slippery, flying, bouncing) and pushes it to exam difficulty. A pentathlon in fact as well as name.
- **Terrain dressing:** the bounce shaft mixes glitter-navy bouncers with plain slabs so the eye must sort them at speed; gold star particles burst where you bounce (feedback = confetti, on-theme for a glitter eraser).
- **Secrets/cages:** `GommesRebond.png` shows a cage sitting on a low ledge INSIDE the bounce shaft — you overshoot it unless you deliberately kill your bounce. Hiding pattern: put the secret where the mechanic's natural rhythm carries you PAST it.
- **Hazard grammar:** in the helico part the corridor itself is the enemy — almost no creatures, just static sharp geometry demanding precise flight (`SuperHelicoImage.png`).

### A5. Level: SPACE MAMA'S CRATER (4 parts)

- **Structure skeleton:** P1 = branching "typical Picture City" map with many secondary pathways and (rare in the game) TWO exit signs — an explicit exploration part. P2 = flying-ring climb over void to upper platforms + bonus level. P3 = the killer: timed jumps across FLYING pencil sharpeners between drawing-pin clusters ("requires perfect timing"; `SpacePirate1.png` shows the tip-down pencil ceiling of this stretch). P4 = final boss rematch on the theatre stage.
- **Mechanic grammar:** moving-platform mastery test; the sharpener from `TailleCrayon.png` (a ridden, flying wedge) is the star object. Note the escalation across the world: sharpener as block (Eraser Plains) → sharpener as lone flying ride → chains of them over void.
- **Boss grammar (both stages):** `SpaceMama.png` — opera-viking woman with a rolling pin next to a washing machine, on a flat desk-wood stage in front of a painted "space crater" backdrop; boss HP shown as a pip-row by her portrait. `SpaceMamaPirate.png` — SAME boss, new costume (pirate with a blunderbuss) in front of a painted beach set. **Law: the boss is an ACTRESS; each bout is a new stage set + costume, arena stays a flat plank stage.** Health pickup (small plate of food) sits on the stage floor. Big slow prop-weapon = readable telegraphs.
- **Enemy grammar:** pirates guard the boss (two must fall before bout 1); the flying blue tentacled imp in `SpacePirate1.png` harasses during platforming — one aerial chaser + static spikes is the standard mix.

## PART B — THE CAVES OF SKOPS (darkness craft + crystal caves)

### B0. World fiction (from `_The_Caves_of_Skops.html`)

A giant water-filled cave system under the valley, framed by an "eerie atmosphere... the sound of water dropping into pools is the only thing to disturb the deadly silence." Entry runs through a friendly alien's snack bar whose power is out — the whole world quest is diegetic electrics: punch the plug, ride the saucer-wire network, relight the sign. Deep parts add lava that rises with tremors. Enemies: dark imps, hunters, stone dogs, spiders, big-mouthed fish; boss = a giant scorpion, asleep until you wake him.

### B1. Darkness handling (the law our night-chapter needs)

- `RaymanLuciole.png` is the master image: the screen is **pure black except one circular light-pool** around the hero. Inside the circle the cave renders in warm amber-browns; the hero's OWN sprite is recolored to the lamp tint (light grades the character, not just the room). The circle edge is softly dithered, radius roughly a third of screen width. Nothing outside it renders at all — no cheating silhouettes.
- Fiction: the diner owner GIVES you the firefly as a gift before the dark part (`JoeIntro.html`/`_Eat_at_Joe's.html` P1 → P2); the light **follows your fist** — so throwing a punch throws your light. Risk and vision are the same resource. That coupling is the genius bit.
- Readability support: inside the pool, hazards keep their color codes (green claw-plants read instantly); the HUD stays lit.
- Contrast craft in the LIT cave parts: three depth layers with strict value separation — near-black red/brown foreground plants overlapping the frame bottom (`Caves1PC.png`), bright teal play-rock in the middle, huge desaturated navy shapes far back. Collectible sparkle-orbs are the brightest pixels on screen and draw the climb line.

### B3. Level: CRYSTAL PALACE (2 parts — deliberately the shortest level)

- **Structure skeleton:** P1 = helicopter + grappling-fist traversal dodging blue spiky balls; P2 = timed jumps across intermittent platforms ("time his jumps... to avoid a potential loss of a life") + bonus level; the only caves level hosting the wandering magician NPC. ### B4. Level: EAT AT JOE'S (6 parts — the masterpiece of diegetic structure)

- **Structure skeleton:** P1 = pure narrative intro at the diner (`JoeIntro.png`: seashell snack bar, bubbly letter sign UNLIT, mushroom stools, chalkboard menu, hookah, barrels — a fully dressed safe-zone diorama); owner hands over the firefly. P2 = the famous dark part (firefly circle). P3 = trap gauntlet, which is the UPPER half of the same map as P5 — the level literally goes out and comes back through the same geography. P4 = punch the plug + first saucer-wire ride; the wiki notes it is one of only three non-boss parts in the whole game with NO cage — the ride IS the reward. P5 = saucer network with direction-levers (lower half of P3's map). P6 = return to the diner across water on buoys, dodging spike-balls and big-mouth fish (`PoissonDents.png`) — and the sign is lit: your work visibly fixed the world.
- **Mechanic grammar:** each part = one system (dark / traps / ride / ride+switches / water), all justified by ONE fiction (restore the diner's electricity). This is the strongest transferable template in the study: a chapter = an errand for a friendly NPC whose place you visibly repair, out-and-back through mirrored map halves.
- **Set-piece:** the treasure alcove (`SpiderStinger.png`) — chest spilling coins, hero sprawled dazed in the pile with a red bat-thing drooping overhead. Reward and ambush in the same alcove.
- **Readability:** in dark parts, danger keeps its daylight color grammar inside the light circle; in lit parts the wire-rails are drawn as thick red-brown twisted strands so the path reads like drawn cable.

### B5. Level: MR SKOPS' STALACTITES (3 parts)

- **Structure skeleton:** P1 = a single huge part containing ALL SIX of the level's cages (one of only three such parts in the game) — a pure secrets-hunt part; the PC version even lets you finish the world by breaking all cages and leaving via the entry sign WITHOUT fighting the boss. P2 = the sleeping scorpion: you cross a bridge of red thorn-blossoms over golden lava toward the visibly sleeping boss (`SkopsDort.png`), wake him, and he attacks — the fight is CAUSED by you. P3 = the boss proper over rising lava.
- **Boss grammar:** `Skops.png` — giant crowned scorpion, one huge gold-padded pincer forward, segmented tail arched over his own head (the strike reads a mile away), on a sandy arena before a night skyline of domed ruins. `SkopsRecule.png` — the boss RETREATS when hit (tug-of-war along a brick parapet over golden lava). `SkopsRigolo.png` — between phases he pulls comic faces (crossed bulging eyes, tongue out): the monster is also a clown. **Law: a boss for children = huge readable silhouette + slow arcing telegraph + comedy beats + a fight you provoked and could feel slightly sorry about** — one step from our redemption rule (we go further: console, never defeat).
- **Cage hiding (both worlds):** `CageOuf.png` — a cage nested INSIDE a monstrous open-jaw structure, approach marked by a sparkle-and-orb trail, dark foreground claws framing it; `GommesRebond.png` — cage below the natural bounce path. Patterns: (1) inside a guarded maw/nook, (2) off the mechanic's momentum line, (3) whole dedicated hunt-part with all six.

## PART C — HERO POSES & HANDS (forensic, from 5x crops in `scratchpad/crops/`)

The hero is limbless: hands are free-floating white gloves, so EVERY pose reads through hand shape + offset alone. Hands are huge — consistently ~50–60% of head height — and the animation swings between exactly three hand states: **ball-fist mitt / relaxed mitt / splayed open glove.** Nothing in between ever renders, which is why it stays readable at 40px tall.

1. **Idle** (`stand_image1pc.png`, from `Image1PC.png`): hands hang as relaxed egg-shaped mitts at hip height, thumbs fused into the mass, zero finger separation. One hand drifts slightly in front of the hip — the float is the personality.
2. **Sprint** (`run_merencre.png`, from `MerEncre.png`): body pitches ~30° forward, hair streams back; both hands ball into knuckle-forward FISTS tucked in at torso sides like a boxer. No fingers at all at speed.
3. **Jump/fall** (`jumpfall_bacencre.png`, from `BacEncre.png`): hands fly OUT wide to shoulder height, open into thick C-crescents — distinct thumb up, remaining fingers one fused curled mass (a pac-man silhouette). Hair whips into a full loop above the head. The open-C reads as "grabbing at air."
4. **Hover/helicopter** (`superheli_pose.png`, from `SuperHelicoImage.png`): the money frame for hands — one glove raised beside the head fully SPLAYED, four fingers + thumb each individually drawn (each finger ~2px at native res), palm to camera, jazz-hand; the far glove mirrors it in shadow tint. Spinning hair is drawn as a solid golden halo-ellipse (motion smear as object). Splay = maximum-effort telegraph.
5. **Punch wind-up** (`punch_skopsrecule.png`, from `SkopsRecule.png`): both fists spheres at chest guard height, elbow-less; body squares up.
6. **Punch flight** (`punch_cageouf.png`, from `CageOuf.png`): the fist DETACHES and sails as a white sphere with visible knuckle bumps, dragging cyan sparkles + white speed streaks; the body leans after it, eyes locked on target, and the remaining arm-slot is just... empty. Fist ball ≈ 45% of head. The projectile-fist with a sparkle trail is the single most game-feel-dense sprite in the corpus.
7. **Crouch-crawl** (`crouch_tube.png`, from `TubeGouache.png`): torso squashes to a ball and BOTH gloves plant on the floor ahead as load-bearing feet — front glove fingers splayed on the ground (individually readable), rear glove knuckles-down. The hands become locomotion. Free idea for our rig: in crawl, hands do the walking.
8. **Cautious tiptoe** (`tiptoe_skopsdort.png`, from `SkopsDort.png`): upright, one glove forward one back at waist height, both HALF-open (fingers slightly curled, ready to grab), oversized eye swiveled toward the sleeping boss. Half-open = alert.
9. **Bounce recoil** (`bounce_gommes.png`, from `GommesRebond.png`): body tips diagonally off-axis, one glove trails behind as an open crescent, and a shoe visibly lags/separates below the body — on impacts the loose parts LAG, selling squash without deforming any limb (there are none).
10. **Dazed** (`SpiderStinger.png`): sprawled flat in the coin pile, effects circling the head; hands vanish under the body — defeat = hands gone.
11. **Lamp-lit** (`lit_luciole.png`, from `RaymanLuciole.png`): whole sprite re-tinted amber inside the light circle — pose grammar unchanged, palette obeys the light source.

**Hand-rework takeaways for our rig:** (a) three hand states only, swapped hard — fist / mitt / splay; (b) hands orbit the body and LEAD every action by a frame or two (out before jump apex, forward before punch); (c) splayed fingers are reserved for maximum effort or balance, which makes them feel loud; (d) fist-as-projectile + particle trail = attack readability at any size; (e) in crouch the hands touch ground and take weight; (f) hand ≈ half a head is the readability floor at small sprite sizes.

## PART D — THE TEN TRANSFERABLE LAWS (for the Painted Book)

1. **Admit the artifice.** The stationery world works because it never hides that it's a picture; stage-set bosses, framed paintings, graffiti on the desk. Our book should love being a book (margins, grid squares, red-pen marks as world features).
2. **One object = one verb.** Every stationery item has exactly one platforming meaning (pin=spike, glitter=bounce, ink=slip/death, sharpener=moves). Vocabulary before sentences: introduce each object alone, then compose.
3. **Escalate the same object across the world** (sharpener: block → lone flying ride → timed chains over void), instead of adding new objects.
4. **Material tells condition:** the hazard state of a surface is painted ON it (ink stain = slippery). No floating UI needed.
5. **Story on the sides, gameplay on the tops:** decorate vertical faces and undersides; keep walkable tops clean; outline the play plane, wash the background, silhouette the foreground (three-value depth, `Caves1PC.png`).
6. **Darkness = a given light-pool that follows your ACTION resource** (light rides the fist): vision and risk share one economy; the lamp is a gift from a friend, not a pickup.
7. **Chapter = an errand that visibly repairs a friend's place, out-and-back through mirrored map halves** (the diner: dark → traps upper path → plug + wire-ride back along the lower path → lit sign at return).
8. **Secrets live off the momentum line:** below the bounce path, inside the guarded maw, or in one dedicated all-cages hunt part; approach marked by sparkle trails.
9. **Boss = actor:** flat stage, painted backdrop swaps, costume changes, huge slow prop telegraphs, comic faces between phases, pip-row health — and a fight the hero provoked (we convert this to console-and-befriend).
10. **Hands carry the acting:** three hand states, hands lead the motion, splay = effort, detached-fist-with-trail = attack feedback, lagging loose parts = impact. (Full detail in Part C.)

## 1. CANDY CHÂTEAU — the finale level ("Mr Dark's Dare")

### 1.1 Structure: one world = one level, four parts, each a different *kind* of game
From the saved level page (`candy-chateau/_Mr_Dark's_Dare.html`), confirmed by the screenshots:

1. **Part 1 — vehicle slide** (`Chateau1PC.png`): the hero rides a kitchen pan like a sled down slopes of whipped cream over chocolate. Movement is momentum-based; hazards are gaps and pools of colored syrup ("sweet lakes"). The Jaguar build replaced this with a cloud-riding gauntlet past folding-knife and corkscrew hazards (`CCJag4.PNG`, `CCJag8.PNG`) — evidence the *phase slot* matters more than its specific content.
2. **Part 2 — mirror-clone chase** (`BadRayman.png`): the villain conjures a dark copy of the hero that repeats the player's every input on a delay; touching it kills. The screenshot shows the clone one ledge above-behind on the cake terrain. This weaponizes the player's own full move-set — every mechanic learned all game is now also the threat.
3. **Part 3 — spell gauntlet** (`CourseForcee.png` = "forced run"): a sequence of curses — reversed controls, then a forced-run section with the glide disabled, then the thrown-fist attack is *stolen* at the end. Mechanics are subtracted one at a time, the exact inverse of the game's power-grant progression.
4. **Part 4 — boss** (`MrDark.png` + the five background plates): the villain fights first with projectiles, then morphs into fusion creatures composed of the game's earlier bosses (per the level page: a scorpion/stone fusion, a mosquito/saxophone/opera-lady fusion, etc.). The finale is a literal **remix of everything**: your moves (part 2), your controls (part 3), and every boss you beat (part 4).

**Law to take:** the final chapter re-tests the whole curriculum by *recombining* it — chase using all movement, gauntlet that removes powers one by one, boss that quotes every earlier setpiece. Nothing new is taught in the finale; everything old is re-armed.

### 1.2 Terrain dressing — dessert as geology
Across `BadRayman.png`, `Chateau1PC.png`, `CourseForcee.png`, `CCJag4.PNG`, `CCJag8.PNG`:

- **Walkable ground is always a *food slab* with a *frosting crest*.** Pink sponge-cake ledges studded with embedded golden candied drops (`BadRayman.png`); dark chocolate blocks with a waffle/scallop relief (`Chateau1PC.png`, `CCJag4.PNG`); floating shards of broken chocolate bar as jump platforms (`CourseForcee.png`). The top edge of every platform gets a bright cream lip — the same job snow caps do on mountain ledges — so the walkable line reads instantly.
- **Cream = snow, syrup = water.** Whipped-cream drifts form slopes and crests; glossy teal syrup pools are the drowning hazard (`CCJag4.PNG`, `CCJag8.PNG` show a teal glaze layer between cream banks). One material vocabulary, hazard/safe distinction by gloss: matte cream is safe, shiny glaze kills.
- **Background props are the same food at giant scale:** a flan the size of a house wearing a floppy beret, an ice-cream cone with a starred pink swirl, a sprinkled doughnut, striped candy-cane columns (`CourseForcee.png`, `BadRayman.png` right edge). Scale inflation of ordinary objects is what makes the world read "bewitched," not new inventions.
- **Sky:** marbled purple-pink storm clouds in every shot — the sugar world sits under a bruised, uneasy sky. Sweet terrain + ominous sky is the tension chord of the whole world.

### 1.3 Boss arena shape
From `MrDark.png` (gameplay) and the five background plates (`Mr_Dark_Background_PC.png`, `_PS1_EU`, `_PS1_Japan`, `_Saturn_EU`, `_Saturn_Japan`) plus `Rayman_Saturn_Moons.png`:

- **A single-screen flat-floored rotunda** — no platforms at all. The floor is a shallow bowl of purple stone, and the *entire floor is a mosaic portrait of the villain's face* (hat, mustache-swirl, yellow eyes) — you fight him while standing on his image.
- **The walls are a memory gallery:** on PC/PS1 the curved back wall holds five arched stained-glass windows, each a portrait of one earlier world's boss (green serpent, gold saxophone, pink-green masked face, blue mustached mask, crowned purple bird). The arena literally displays the "all previous chapters" theme of the fight. The Saturn plates (`Mr_Dark_Background_Saturn_*.png`) replace the windows with open arches to a night sky with a full moon — same architecture, emptier and lonelier. Per the level page, the Saturn windows glow behind when hit and **shatter when the boss falls** — the gallery itself is the victory effect.
- **Two hanging ropes** flank the room (`MrDark.png`, rope visible again top-left of `Rayman_Saturn_Moons.png`) — the room's only interactive furniture, and the villain enters holding one: theatrical, circus-like.
- **A carved frieze band with invented glyph-letters** runs at eye height around the whole curve on every plate — decoration that implies language without being readable. Good trick for a book-world: fake letterforms as ornament.
- `Rayman_Saturn_Moons.png` shows the fight mid-chaos: small pale crescent/star objects scattered across the floor around the hero — the boss's projectiles litter the arena rather than vanishing, dressing the floor with the fight's own history.

**Arena laws:** boss room = one flat screen, no help, villain-themed floor, walls that narrate the game's past, exactly one or two interactive props, and the room itself performs the victory (windows shatter).

## 2. WORLD MAP — the overworld

Sources: `overview/CarteRayman1.gif` (smaller/day-blue variant), `overview/R1-PCWorldMap.png` (night, moon top-left), `overview/R1PS1WorldMap.png` (identical painting; the moon is replaced by a huge floating pink ribbon-wrapped object, balloon-like).

- **The whole game is ONE painted island seen from one fixed viewpoint.** Not a node graph, not a scrolling map: a single landscape painting in which each world is a *geological region*. Forest = dense green canopy with palms and a waterfall at bottom-left (sea level). Music = a cloud bank floating above it, drawn with a musical staff swirling through the sky, a cloud-meadow shelf holding a giant drum, a tuba, and a cliff edge made of piano keys, plus a pink bandstand pavilion on the clouds. Mountains = tall lavender rock pinnacles in the center, the tallest crowned by a tiny statue-like figure. Caves = the brown-pink crag face on the right, drawn with mushroom-shelf ledges, cave mouths, and a small red glowing sign at one opening (an inhabited hole — a joke landmark). Candy = the summit region at top-right: cottages with cream roofs, a doughnut, cookie ledges, pink frosting hills. A tiny flagged boat sits on the sea at bottom-center.
- **Progression is drawn as altitude.** You read the campaign bottom-left → up through the clouds → over the peaks → into the right-hand crags → ending at the candy summit. "How far am I?" = "how high on the painting am I?" No numbers needed.
- **Each world advertises itself with 2–3 oversized prop-landmarks** (piano-key cliff, giant tuba; mushroom shelves, neon cave sign; cake cottages). A world = a color field + silhouette landmarks, legible at thumbnail size.
- **The two versions differ only in sky object** (moon vs. floating pink parcel) — the landscape painting is the invariant; the sky is where a version/story flag can hang.

**Map laws for us:** one continuous painting, worlds as regions not icons, progression as a readable physical climb, each region owning a color + two landmark props, sky slot reserved for story-state.

## 3. HUD & GAME-FEEL SURFACE

Consistent across every gameplay shot (`BadRayman.png`, `CCJag4.PNG`, `CCJag8.PNG`, `Foret1PC.png`, `Ciel1PC.png`, `Montagnes1PC.png`, `Caves1PC.png`, `Image1PC.png`, `Chateau1PC.png`, `Rayman_Saturn_Moons.png`, `MoskitoFight2.png`, `MrSax.png`, `Skops.png`, `SpaceMama.png`):

- **Exactly two corner clusters, nothing else.** Top-left: the hero's smiling face as a portrait icon, "×NN" lives beside it, and a short row of health pips *under* it (3 larger red/yellow dots on PC, 5 small red beads on PS1/Saturn — `Rayman_Saturn_Moons.png` vs `BadRayman.png`). Top-right: one sparkling blue orb icon + "×NN" for the collectible count. That is the *entire* permanent HUD — no score, no timer, no minimap, no buttons.
- **No panels or bars behind the HUD** — icons and hand-drawn numerals float directly over the painted scene. The numerals are chunky, tilted, cartoon-outlined — they belong to the art style, not to an OS.
- **Health is on the portrait, not in a bar:** damage state lives as pips attached to the hero's face icon, keeping "how am I doing" and "who am I" in one glance spot.
- **Boss fights add exactly one element:** a horizontal pill-shaped meter at the *bottom-left*, a frame holding a row of pips that drain (gold pips in a red frame in `MoskitoFight2.png` and `SpaceMama.png`; a fine-toothed white comb of segments in `MrSax.png` and `Skops.png`). Player status stays top, boss status bottom — vertical separation, same pip language for both.
- **Everything diegetic beyond that:** power-grants are shown by the fairy appearing in-scene (`BetillaPoingGrappin.png`), progress by the map painting, story by full-screen art (`Rayman1Group.PNG`). The HUD never explains; the scene does.

**HUD laws:** two corners only; portrait+pips for the player; count+icon for the one collectible; boss meter bottom-left, pips not bars; zero background plates; numerals drawn in the game's own hand.

## 4. HERO CHARACTER STUDY — the HAND DOSSIER (priority)

The hero is a limbless figure: bare oval hands and shoes that float near an egg-shaped torso, big head with heavy banana-lobed hair, red neck-scarf. Because there are no arms, **the hands ARE the acting** — every pose statement is made by where the two gloves sit. Read from 4× crops of: `Poing.png` (punch), `CourseForcee.png` (leap), `BadRayman.png` (run + clone), `Foret1PC.png` (walk), `MrDark.png` (floored), `Rayman_Saturn_Moons.png` (arms-up), `CCJag4.PNG` (idle at edge), `Chateau1PC.png` (sled), `MoskitoFight2.png` (frontal run), `Image1PC.png` (stride), plus the artworks `RaymanBox.png`, `Rayman_Cheat_Artwork_2.png`, `Rayman1Group.PNG`, `BetillaPoingGrappin.png` and the designer's construction sketch `RaymanLimbs.jpg`.

### 4.1 Construction facts (from `RaymanLimbs.jpg` + `RaymanBox.png`)
- The designer's pencil sketch builds each extremity as an **independent simple volume** — hand = one oval with finger bumps, foot = one bean — placed by eye around the torso circle. There is no wrist, no cuff seam to hide: the glove just ends in a rounded heel.
- Gloves are **four-fingered, puffy, pillow-like**; knuckle creases only appear in large art (`RaymanBox.png`), never in sprites.
- **Size ratio:** in key art the hand is large — roughly 60–70 % of head height (`RaymanBox.png`: the thumbs-up fist nearly matches the nose+mouth mass). In-game sprites shrink it to roughly ⅓–½ of head height (`Foret1PC.png` crop). Rule: the more emotional the image, the bigger the hands.
- **Feet ground, hands act.** Feet almost always stay under the torso's plumb line; hands roam. The silhouette stays balanced because the shoes anchor it.

### 4.3 The design language distilled (rules for our own character)

1. **Open palm = feeling, fist = doing.** Open spread fingers appear at emotional peaks (victory, greeting, appeal to viewer); fists appear for action (run lead hand, punch, thumbs-up). The relaxed curl-mitt is the neutral state between them. A pointing finger is a poster-only gesture.
2. **Distance = intensity meter.** Neutral poses keep each hand within about one hand-width of the torso; excitement pushes them out; the punch (hand leaves entirely) and the victory (hands a head above the head) are the two extremes of the same dial. Animate emotion by *moving hands away from the body*, not by changing the body.
3. **Connectedness without arms is achieved by four devices, all visible in the crops:**
   - **Silhouette overlap:** in most frames at least one hand overlaps the torso or hip outline (`CCJag4.PNG`, `BadRayman.png` run) so hand+body read as one blob;
   - **Shared lighting:** the far-side hand is always painted a step darker/grayer than the near hand (`Foret1PC.png`, `BadRayman.png`) — the pair obeys one light source with the body, welding them into one figure;
   - **One motion arc:** hands sit on the same curve that the lean of the torso, the streaming hair, and the scarf tail describe (`Poing.png`: nose, hair, chest-lean and flying fist all on one line). Hair and scarf are the arc's flags; the hands are beads on it;
   - **Tuck-in under stress:** in compact/ballistic states (sled `Chateau1PC.png`, leap `CourseForcee.png`) the hands merge into the body mass entirely — the design never leaves small parts floating far away *unless that distance is the statement*.
4. **Feet are the anchor, hands the voice.** Shoes stay under the center of mass in nearly every frame; only the floored pose (`MrDark.png`) lets gloves touch ground, and then they visibly flatten and take weight.
5. **Identity survives recolor:** the dark clone (`BadRayman.png` crop) is the identical silhouette with a swapped palette (magenta/blue body, grey gloves, yellow eyes) — proof the silhouette, not the color, carries the character. Our girl-rig and any "bewitched" variant can rely on the same law.
6. **Hands are shared species language:** the fairy, the finale friends (`Rayman1Group.PNG`: several companions raise the same white gloves), and even the villain use floating white gloves — one world-wide anatomy rule, differently sized.

## 5. CAGES, MAGICIAN, BONUS SCREENS, SIGNS — what the set contains

## The PR-B hand-choreography spec seeded by this pass

1. **One-active-hand law** (from the original's frames): at most ONE hand gestures;
   the other rests as a fist near the hip or tucks behind the body silhouette.
2. **Size**: glove ≈ ⅔ of head height, never near head-sized.
3. **Jump**: front fist punches forward-up at chest height; back fist trails low
   behind — never both overhead.
4. **Fall**: front hand opens (spread), back fist stays; asymmetric heights (done
   in #219, keep).
5. **Hang**: both grips ON the ledge lip corner (offset toward the wall side), body
   hangs off-axis below them.
6. **Swing**: the grip pair sits ALONG the rope direction (rotate toward anchor);
   body tilts with the pendulum angle.
7. **Vine**: hands alternate (one at brow height, one a half-cell higher), body
   offset ~4px from the vine axis so the vine never bisects the face.
8. **Hover**: front hand reaches UP toward the quill-rotor (grip), back fist at hip.
9. **Stale hint**: footer control line still says "↑ hochziehen" — replace with the
   real verbs (hang: jump to leap up, DOWN to drop).
