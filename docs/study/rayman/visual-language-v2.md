## §1 · THE CROSS-WORLD LAWS — the grammar that held in EVERY world studied

### 1.1 Background architecture
- **4–6 depth bands, separated by hue shift + lightness lift — never desaturation.** Forest
  day stack: foreground occluder → play plane → water plane → SATURATED purple/pink mid-band
  → pale blue-white peaks → sky (`01/03_forest-parallax-floating-island.png`). Music world:
  lavender sky → clouds → two-green hills → hot pink flora, backgrounds stay saturated even
  at night — value drops, hue survives (`04/00`, `04/08`). Stationery world: 3–4 planes
  separated by value, wash-only backgrounds, zero outlines (`10_paintbrush-enemies.png`,
  `Image1PC.png`). Confirms level-anatomy corrected belief 3.
- **Two closure modes** (confirms corrected belief 1): low paths seal the screen top with a
  near-black canopy/rock band with hanging fringes (`02/01_electoon-cage.png`, cave teeth
  `04_dark-purple-cave.png` [CoS]); high/open paths dissolve upward into haze + sky
  (`03/08_lagoon-water-crossing.png`, Gong Heights `05/04`).
- **One translucent colored VEIL layer per world, in the world's signature hue**, hung between
  sky and silhouettes: magenta aurora curtains in the crystal night (`07/00`, `07/08`), teal
  streaked drapes in the caves (`01_crystal-palace-teal-cave.png`), pink aurora ribbons over
  the boss void (`06/00`–`06/04` band-land). The veil is the world's fingerprint.
- **Hue re-key per segment is the progress bar.** Day → dusk → night-storm inside one level
  (`04/00→04/07→04/08`); approach-heat as progress (teal hunt → full red wash at the den,
  `01`/`02` [CoS boss level]); the forest darkens level by level (wiki `_The_Dream_Forest`).
- **Sub-zones invert the world's two hues**, never introduce a third system: purple cave
  pocket with teal pillars inside the teal forest (`02/06_pink-bird-enemy.png`); night parts
  recolor the SAME terrain vocabulary night-blue rather than swapping it (`09/01` picture
  city).
- **Bonus rooms drop the painting**: flat/abstract void backdrops + relocated HUD mark reward
  space as "outside" the fiction (`01/02_magician-secret-level-orange.png`,
  `RaymanPC_-_Jungle_21.png`). Cross-ref cookbook §10.

### 1.2 Terrain mass language
- **Thick mass under every top** (confirms corrected belief 2): crust:body ≥ 1:2 in the
  forest (lit moss crust + drip fringe over cobble body, `13/00`, `01/03`); drum bodies run
  to frame bottom, even floating ribbons get a colored underside stripe (`04/01`, `05/01`);
  food slabs are thick with the walk-line as the lightest stripe (`01` candy, `02`
  `BadRayman.png`). Thin hard-edged shelves are legal ONLY where thin is the theme
  (stationery world eraser strips, `02` picture-city) — and then both unsupported ends and
  the hazard beneath are shown in one frame.
- **Walk-top plain, rim ornate** — the single strongest readability edge treatment: matte
  cream drum tops with ALL decoration on the vertical faces (`04/01`, `04/03`, `05/07`);
  standable lips carry a bright moss/teal strip in the mountains (`07/00`, `07/02`); the
  cream lip on quilted chocolate (`01` candy). Hero and hazards always sit against the plain
  band.
- **Walkable-vs-decor is a color contract**: lime crust = stand, purple/pink/red = decorative
  or hostile, blue = water, grey = body/wall (forest, `01/03`, `02/07`); no walkable surface
  lacks the crust, no decoration wears it. Decor that competes with footing fails art QA
  (cookbook §6).
- **Terrain-as-creature is a material statement**: stone men and stone dogs are built from
  the same slab vocabulary as the cliffs (`HommedePierre.png`, `ChiensdePierre.png`); trunk-
  beasts ARE the floor (`Tronc.png`); camouflage included — find the eyes (§1.4).
- **Undersides are gravity-marked** (noduled, dripping) so up-vs-down of any platform reads
  instantly (`06`, `07` [CoS]; drip fringes forest-wide).

### 1.2b ★ THE TOPIC-MATERIAL LAW (Koki, dossier-gate amendment 2026-07-23 — BINDING)

**A chapter's terrain vocabulary is built FROM the unit's topic material — never from
generic nature surfaces.** The studied game does exactly this: the music world's ground is
drums, cymbals and staves (`04/01`, `05/07`); the stationery world's ground is erasers, ink
blocks and pencils (`01`–`02` picture-city); the food world's ground is cake and icing
(`01` candy). Nowhere does a themed world fall back to "earth with grass" — the THEME is
the geology. For us:

- **ch01 (school things):** masses = stacked books/cardboard, platforms = paper sheets and
  ruler beams, ramps = leaning book covers, slide = chalk dust on a board edge, pits = ink.
  Grass/earth/ice crusts are BANNED in ch01 — the old grass-crust + earth-fill + "icy"
  chalk vocabulary is retired with the rebuild.
- **Every later chapter re-derives its geology from ITS unit** (zoo → enclosure timber,
  straw bales, pawprint flagstones; pirates → planks, rigging, sailcloth; feelings →
  see the chapter sheet; band → the music-world steal becomes nearly literal).
- The painted BOOK page (cream ground, ruled lines, paper grain) stays the universal
  BACKDROP substrate — the law governs what terrain is MADE OF, not the page behind it.
- Cross-law: affordance casting (1.3) still governs BEHAVIOR (soft=stand, sharp=hurt);
  this law governs MATERIAL. Together: every surface is topic-material AND behaves like
  what it is.

### 1.3 AFFORDANCE CASTING — the anti-"random placement" law (fullest treatment)
The stationery world is the premise's closest cousin: **every solid object's game role
restates the real object's property as a child knows it.** Soft = stand (erasers),
liquid-that-stains = kill/slip (ink), sharp = spike (pins, nibs), glitter = bounce, the only
machine = the only mover (sharpener), long = beam or stalactite (pencils) (`01`–`07`, `11`,
`12` picture-city; level-anatomy A1 table). Five sub-laws, each frame-verified:

1. **Nothing solid is semantically silent.** A prop with no gameplay meaning is either debris
   produced by the world's own verbs (pencil shavings, ink splats, tape scraps — `01`, `03`,
   `06`, `11`) or outline-free backdrop wash. Verb-debris is the cheapest authenticity trick
   in the corpus.
2. **State flips over new assets.** The same pencil is column (up), platform (flat), spike
   (tip-down); the same ball is helper or hazard by ONE added feature (the spike,
   `YinYang.png`); the same eraser is ground / slippery (+ink decal) / bouncy (+glitter).
   Condition = decal, never full recolor — the base identity stays visible around the splat
   (`01` right slab). Variety by state, coherence by construction. [C-9 escalate one object]
3. **Hazards get furniture.** Pins fill checkered boxes held up by chalk sticks (`Craies.png`,
   `02`, `11`); pressure cookers come in rows on a stage (`09/06`); the container is the
   telegraph AND the proof someone arranged this — the exact difference between "authored"
   and "random."
4. **A reserved kill-color, never spent on decor.** Glossy black = death (ink); chromatic
   liquid = paint = ambience (`MerEncre.png`, `PotEncre.png`). Because the codes are never
   spent on décor, décor can be lavish without confusing anyone.
5. **Small closed vocabulary, exam-scheduled.** ~10 terrain words per world; each introduced
   once in isolation, then recombined, then examined at speed (Eraser Plains → Pencil
   Pentathlon structure, wiki). Novelty budget: one new surface per part. [C-2]

The other worlds obey the same casting: drums bounce, trumpets launch, instruments are
architecture (band-land §1.2); sweets sort safe/deadly by finish (candy §1.5); the diner's
buoys advertise the diner (`08_beach-ball-section.png` [CoS]). **Our doc-31 analogue:
eraser=soft ground, ink=kill/slip, nib/compass=spike, ruler=beam, sharpener=mover.**

### 1.4 Eyes = "this acts"
Everything animate or interactive carries big cartoon eyes; static decor never does. Bongo
platforms, cymbals, mallets, wrong notes (`04/01`, `06/00`, `HittingStick.png`); flying picks
and spiky balls (`Pieu.png`, `02_crystal-stone-face.png`); the eyed saucer vehicle
(`Soucoupe.png` [CoS]); winged rings drawn as creatures so they read grabbable (`03/11`
forest); pre-boss cutlery that WATCHES (`05` candy). Corollary: anything airborne gets drawn
wings/propellers — mobility is drawn, never implied (`09/07` winged scatter, `03/09` winged
one-up).

### 1.5 Gloss / hue hazard grammar
- **Gloss is a warning channel, matte is safe.** Slippery = high-gloss cool (ice-blue/mint
  body + continuous white specular top-streak + dark underside stripe, `05/01`, `04/02`);
  grippy = warm matte (cream drum skin). The two never share a hue (band-land §3). Candy:
  matte cream slope = safe, glossy teal lake = death (`CCJag4.PNG`). Ink: black + wet
  highlight + spiked meniscus = lethal, no signage (`01` picture-city, `MerEncre.png`).
- **Glow = danger or hot interaction at night; warm-flat = goal.** Lava rocks glow with crack
  lines, spiky balls sparkle, gem studs glint on spike beds; exit signs and friendly objects
  are flat warm gold (`07/01`, `07/08`, `07/10`; blue-mountains B1). At night the luminosity
  budget IS the readability system. (Our engine reserves glow for collectible/hint — cookbook
  §6 — so hostile "glow" must translate to self-luminous PAINT, not the engine glow channel.)
- **Emotion painted on terrain = rule painted on terrain**: red crystals wear a scowl (hazard
  with one safe affordance — the crown, `Rubis.png`), blue crystals droop sad faces
  (`Soucoupe.png` [CoS]) — a two-axis color+expression material-state code, native to our
  redeemed-enemy face language.
- **Hazard families share silhouette language with enemy families**: colored quill-spikes
  shaped like the wrong-notes' own tails (`05/08`), so one read covers both.

### 1.6 Enemy staging + silhouette
- **One foe per ledge step, at hero height, silhouette-first** (`02/04` forest; blue-mountains
  dogs at ankle height on ledges). Turret/ranged threats sit EMBEDDED in terrain at crossing
  lips — area denial, not pursuit (tentacle-flower at the far lip of the water gap `01/06`;
  lightning-eye between platforms `04/09`; throwers arcing into the jump lane
  `DeuxHommesPierre.png`).
- **Threats patrol BETWEEN safe points** to force rhythm-reading, not reflex (fish mid-gap
  between buoys, `PoissonDents.png` [CoS]; piranha beside the nenuphar, `Piranha.png`).
- **Ambush ON the prize punishes greed at the moment of reward** (thread-spider over the open
  treasure chest, `SpiderStinger.png`) — banked as staging grammar; our economy inverts the
  cruelty per [A-8]/[A-20].
- **Size ladder per world, ~0.3× to ~4–5×**, capped by the boss (mini-notes → wrong notes →
  moth → red monster → sax, band-land §5; little Livingstone → tall → Bzzit → Moskito,
  forest §2.4).
- **Color-contrast law**: foes carry warm/dark accents against the cool backdrop; nothing
  hostile wears the walkable color (`02/04`, forest §2.4). Creatures are round and mobile;
  object-hazards are angular and fixed (picture-city B5) — threat sorting by shape class.
- **The hazard's whole body is its telegraph**: the mallet's long rest pose (`04/03`,
  `HittingStick.png`); the scorpion's tail arched over his own head (`Skops.png`) —
  anatomical telegraphs, not VFX. [C-4, A-13]

### 1.7 Interlock set-piece staging [C-3]
The most-praised trait of the original: movement + scenery + creature + reward locking into
one readable moment. Frame-proven exemplars, one per flavor:
- **Enemy as key**: Livingstones parked on pillars under hanging plums — punch the plum, it
  pins the foe and becomes the stepping stone (`ExplorateursPrunes.png`).
- **Granted verb locked to a set-piece timer**: plant-a-flower vs rising flood in a vertical
  shaft — the scenery is the clock (`03/02_flower-platform-rising-water.png`).
- **Circuit legible before riding**: plug → cable-rail → eyed saucer → red ball-levers ON the
  wire; every interactive element a distinct silhouette on one connected line
  (`06`/`07` [CoS], `SoucoupeLevier.png`).
- **Person-as-platform with a visual punchline**: the monk's eyed bongos become his spectacles
  when the drums meet (`05/05`, `MoineBongosHaut.png`) — the direct ancestor of favor-powers.
- **Pursuit eats the floor**: the chaser shoves a screen-filling spiky fruit that physically
  consumes safe space (`MoskitoPoursuite.png`).
- **Trust-inversion of one asset**: the red scowling crystal is hazard-to-avoid in the dark
  level, then the ONLY bridge over lava at the boss approach (`05`/`06` vs `02`,
  `SkopsDort.png` [CoS]) — the world teaches distrust, then demands you override it. Use with
  [A-2] fairness: first sight survivable.

### 1.8 Collectible / cage / checkpoint signposting
- **Trails are trajectories**: collectible arcs trace the intended jump, swing, or bounce line
  before the player commits (`03/11` ring-swing arc; `05/00` off-ribbon constellation; gem
  ladder marking the bounce flight `05` picture-city). The devs even write words in gems
  (`11_no-written-in-gems.png`). An orb cluster off the obvious path = a secret is there.
  Cross-ref cookbook §2 "trails are sentences."
- **Light shafts point**: diagonal background rays fall where something matters (`02/07`
  beside the cage; green misty shaft in the boss arena `03/10`).
- **Rewards stack over targets**: the golden-fist disc hovers directly above a cage (`02/05`);
  bait doubles as signpost (`03/09` winged one-up past the flytrap — with [A-8]: bait never
  over an unmarked kill zone).
- **Cages**: 3/4-view bar-cage, gold padlock, captives visibly squirming; placed in guarded
  nooks, past climbs, at trail ends, lit by shafts (`13/01`, `02/01`, `CageOuf.png`); freed
  captive celebrates on a podium (`13/01`). Distribution is lumpy by design; three parts in
  the whole game hold all six (`_Twilight_Gulch` P1, `_Mr_Sax's_Hullaballoo` P1,
  `_Mr_Skops'_Stalactites` P1 — the three reports enumerate each other).
- **Checkpoint = a friendly character**, not a marker (photographer + cutout board, `02/00`).
- **Progress is worn by the map**: level coins fill with faces, complete = smiley medallion
  (`02/08`); per-level legibility exists, within-level location is blind — our build keeps
  the medallion AND adds the inner tier (dream-forest §3.12; cookbook §3 letters-HUD).
- **Landmark-by-anomaly**: surreal one-off props mark secret-adjacent spots (the broom + eyed
  stalactite, `Balai.png`; stone monkey-idol, `01/09`).

### 1.9 Grant-ceremony staging (calm registers)
Two registers, both total isolation from threat and clutter:
- **In-world grant**: flat bare ledge, empty sky, a signpost, the giver, one tutorial line —
  nothing else on screen (`02/02_betilla-fairy-grants-power.png`; helicopter on a bare ledge
  `05/03`; run grant under a starry sky `07/11`, `BetillaCourse.png`).
- **Cutscene grant (gratitude)**: the world drops away to a black card with one portrait +
  caption (`07/07_super-helicopter-grant.png`).
Grant stages are single-purpose and consumed — visited once, removed from replays (wiki
Replays; dream-forest §3.4). Favor-grants are staged in a safe village tableau first: help →
craft → card (`Musicien.png` → `MusicienContent.png` → grant card; the musician favor recasts
the hero's DESTRUCTIVE verb as craft — boulder punched into a new guitar). The very next
scene after a grant examines ONLY the new verb (Gong Heights confidence test, `05/04`).

### 1.10 HP / HUD conventions
- **Two corner clusters only** (level-anatomy law, now six-world-verified): portrait + lives +
  HP pips top-left; one collectible count + icon top-right (`01/01`, all band-land frames).
- **Hero pip color law: gold = current HP, red = empty slot** (`01/01` 3/3 gold; `01/02` 2
  gold + 1 red; `02/06` 4 gold + 1 red).
- **Boss health looks categorically different from hero health**: bottom-left row of red/white
  pips or striped segments FRONTED BY THE BOSS'S OWN FACE icon, appearing only when the fight
  starts (`06/02` sax 12-segment striped bar; `04` scorpion red pips [CoS]; `08`–`10` candy
  fusions; both bouts of the actress incl. her bout-1 costume `08/09`). Ownership of the bar
  is never ambiguous.
- **The HUD never dims** — in the dark level, status stays full-brightness against black
  (`02`, `03` [CoS]). Bonus rooms relocate the HUD to the bottom + house the timer in a prop
  (`01/09`, `03/07`).

### 1.11 Boss-arena grammar
- **Proscenium**: one flat non-scrolling screen, framed and closed — root-weave ring
  (`03/10`), mask-lined dome with a central vertical (`06/03`), torch-flanked ledge whose
  background faces echo the boss silhouette (`07/10`, `MrStone.png`), glyph-banded temple
  (`04` [CoS]). Prop economy ≤ 2 (the finale rotunda: two ropes, candy V14).
- **Chase-then-arena**: the boss is met as an environmental force before the fight — the
  Moskito pursuit (`MoskitoPoursuite.png`), the golem chase with shelter-crevasse rhythm
  (`MrStonePoursuite.png`), the sax chase that PRE-DRAINS up to 9 of 12 HP
  (`MrSaxPoursuite.png`, wiki). Familiarity may also come by early cameo — the sleeping boss
  staged in the level before his arena (`SkopsDort.png`).
- **Counter-verb rehearsed for free**: the punch-a-note-back verb gets a zero-stakes cameo one
  level early (`05/07`, `MrSaxAllegroPresto.png`), then the chase, then the exam — three-stage
  rehearsal makes the fight fair. Arena = exam, never a collectathon: cages live in the exam
  level, zero collection in the boss room (band-land §7; cookbook §3 arena cage sits in or
  AFTER the arena).
- **The fight is provoked, and ends in redemption**: sleeping boss woken (`SkopsDort.png`);
  defeat = a shared dance (sax, wiki); the first boss cries and becomes the mount
  (`_Anguish_Lagoon` — fight → tears → console → befriend → ride). Between-phase clown faces
  reset tension for children (`SkopsRigolo.png`). The redeemed creature and the chapter boss
  can rhyme visually yet must be SEPARATE beings (Bzzit ≠ Moskito).
- **The room performs the fight**: source-boss windows blink per hit, shatter on victory
  (Saturn build, candy §2) — feedback through set dressing, zero UI. Ours inverts shatter
  into restoration (panels bloom).

### 1.12 The finale remix economy
- **Gate before, purity after**: the cage economy is spent at the door (102 cages gate entry);
  the finale hides no new collectibles — only mastery remains (candy V24, L1).
- **Phase-slots, not contents**: one level, four parts, each a different KIND of play built
  from taught verbs — momentum ride / mirror double / subtraction gauntlet / confrontation.
  The port builds swap slot contents while the slots hold (candy V25) — the shape is the
  invariant.
- **Escalation by subtraction and reflection, never addition**: your inputs turned enemy (the
  recolored double — a mirror-threat MUST be palette-differentiated, `BadRayman.png`), your
  controls remapped then removed, powers stripped (fist-theft), history compressed into fused
  quotations (`08`–`10`). Nothing new is taught. [Fairness overrides per A-13/A-17/A-18:
  no instant-death touch, no contradictory-demand pairs, rehearsed strips.]
- **The powerless nadir + captives-repay beat** — the single best structural steal: at the
  lowest point, the stolen power dangles VISIBLY out of reach (the roped glove, `06`/`07`
  candy), and the beings you freed arrive and re-arm you (footage-attested V19). Story and
  mechanic land on the same beat. Ours pays out through 12 classmates + 5 guardians =
  the favor economy where it hurts most.
- **Denouement = the same world recolored calm**: bruise-purple → deep blue, gloss hazards
  gone, warm point-lights as "life restored," one audible life-returns cue (the trumpet-
  flower note, `15/02`); the storyteller who opened the frame closes it (`15/00`).

### 1.13 The antagonist-silhouette law (OSWIN cloak-rule, validated)
The studied villain is carried at EVERY scale by exactly **three primitives: hat brim,
glowing eye-pair, cloak swirl** — closeup (`15/01`), gameplay sprite (`05`/`06` candy), even
the floor mosaic still reads as him (`Mr_Dark_Background_PS1_EU.png`). There is NOTHING under
the eyes — no face, no body, no name in any asset: the design is name-free by construction.
Temperature opposition anchors the cast: warm narrator (gold/purple, round, shares the hero's
body plan = kinship) vs cold antagonist (blue/maroon, angular, hidden) — one pose each
introduces both (`15/00` vs `15/01`). **Rule: give OSWIN a three-primitive silhouette triad
distinctive enough that assets never need a label.**

## §2 · PER-WORLD REGISTERS (and what our chapters steal)

### 2.1 Dream forest — the baseline donor
Two-hue register: teal-cyan interior wall vs green/purple/blue-white day stack; sub-zones
invert the pair (`02/06`). Unique vocabulary: lit moss crust + drip fringe, teardrop islands
with root-tails, lip-flowers, plums, nenuphars, winged rings, bead-sprout pillars (forest
§2.2). **We steal: the whole base grammar** — crust law, closure modes, vine spec (§4), the
redemption template (fight → tears → console → befriend → shared traversal), and one-verb
escalation (the plum's five contexts, §3.5 of the report).

### 2.2 Band-land — feeds p3 (vertical / slide)
Register: lavender-blue day → pink dusk → night storm; high-altitude blue void; salmon sunset
zone. Vocabulary: cream drum-tops with ornate rims, instrument verticals, gloss slide ribbons,
puff-clouds, launchers. **We steal: the slide staging laws** — slides are ROUTES not hazards,
carrying you past dangers; risk is exit control, landing zones are wide and plain; secrets
sit off the fast lane where speed overshoots; the material announces itself (gloss + white
streak + cool hue) and the first slide costs nothing (`04/00` ground level before any void
crossing). Plus: weather as progress bar; the grant-then-solo-exam pattern; pattern-breaks
must be marked ([C-5] vs the trumpet betrayal).

### 2.3 Blue mountains — feeds p2 (night register)
Register: near-black starry sky + magenta veil curtains; full-saturation violet rock, teal
moss lips, pink HUD numerals. Vocabulary: stacked columns/slabs, slab-built creatures, gem-
studded spike beds, glowing lava rocks, eyed picks. **We steal: the night recipe** — value
carries the night, hue carries the charm; same object palette wholesale, only sky + light
re-keyed (day variant proves the dial, `ArbreNoueux.png`); every standable edge keeps a lit
lip; dangerous = self-luminous, friendly = warm-flat. Plus for p3: vertical pressure built
diegetically (rising water below + winched slate above, stoppable by the player's own verb —
`07/09`, `Ravin.png`), shafts framed by parallel columns with vertical streaks as a plumb
line; true forced-scroll at most once.

### 2.4 Picture city — feeds the school world (ch01)
Register: warm desk-wood wall with carved doodles / candy-pastel stroke landscape / night
cloud-sea — all artificial, all readable; boss stages add the theatre flat + curtain.
Vocabulary: the full stationery lexicon (§1.3). **We steal: affordance casting entire** —
the five sub-laws are the anti-randomness engine for our school-supply world; plus the
theatre-boss staging (painted backdrop, plank floor, new costume/set per bout, same
silhouette, big slow domestic props) and the world-doodles-on-itself charm channel
(verb-debris, margin scribbles, gems that spell words).

### 2.5 Caves — feeds the dark rooms (light-contract)
Register: three-value cave stack (near-black fg plants → bright warm play shelf → desaturated
navy faces far); teal→red temperature axis as approach bar. Vocabulary: noduled dripping
shelves, organ-pipe waterfalls, scowling red / sad blue crystals, the electric circuit.
**We steal: the light-pool contract** — (a) pool generous and body-centred (~2.5–3 hero-
heights: feet + landing + one step always lit); (b) inside the pool the daylight palette
holds — darkness crops, never re-skins; (c) the HUD never dims; (d) vision tied to the action
resource (light rides the fist — risk and information share one economy); soft-edged pool
beats hard-edged (`02` vs `RaymanLuciole.png`). ANTI-LAWS banked: dark rooms may hide beauty,
never commitments; never gate completion behind low-information backtracking (caves L3).
Plus: the errand-as-level-list structure with mirrored out-and-back halves ("coming home"),
and the cage-free exhale set-piece at the midpoint.

### 2.6 Candy château — feeds the finale
Register: pastel food masses under a bruised purple sky; the rotunda; the restored night
forest. Vocabulary: cream/nougat/chocolate slab families, gloss lakes, watching cutlery,
scale-inflated sweets. **We steal: the whole finale economy** (§1.12) plus the narrating
arena — floor bears the antagonist's self-image (repainting it = the forgiveness image),
walls carry panels of the freed cast (our stained-glass), a frieze of invented book-glyphs
(letterforms that imply language without being one — born for a book world), ≤2 props,
room reacts per phase.

## §4 · CLIMB / VINE SPEC + MOVEMENT-VISUAL NOTES (feeds the pose program)

**The vine spec (verbatim-faithful from the dream-forest report):**
- Canonical climb surface (`02/01_electoon-cage.png`): a thick tan/beige liana, gently
  S-curved, hanging from a canopy or rock mass; paired green leaflets stud it at regular
  intervals (reading as grip rhythm); loose purple petal-blades attach along it for
  silhouette flair. Warm tan against the cool teal wall = maximum read.
- The hero grips with both hands, body swung slightly off the vine axis (the swingLean read),
  feet free.
- Vines always hang from something DRAWN — a canopy mass, a branch, never off-screen nothing
  (`02/00`, `BetaEnemies.jpg`).
- Climbing is an UP-INTO-THE-ROOF move: the vine is the one warm element that pierces the
  dark top-closure band (wiki: lianas reach the canopy).
- If a deadly vine exists it changes BOTH hue and silhouette (magenta + paired barbs,
  `Lianepiquante.png` — spin-off only; the main game keeps climb surfaces safe). Never a
  recolor.
- Adjacent grip-object: the swing ring is drawn as a creature (wings + eyes) so it reads
  alive/grabbable (`03/11`); its collectible arc pre-draws the swing trajectory.

**Movement-visual notes banked by the reports:**
- Hands: the hand dossier in level-anatomy governs (handStemsFor front/back, one-open-hand
  law, run pump, tightrope hover, lip grips, swingLean, far hand darker 0.62×) — nothing in

  out, `03/05`).

  is drawn in the pose, not just the physics.

  gag state `10/06`).

  ride on plum and saucer (`03/07`, `07` [CoS]).
- Launch/impact feedback: clean projectile return = zigzag flash on the projectile + gold
  spark at the target (`06/02`); punch carries a gold fist flash (`05/06`).
